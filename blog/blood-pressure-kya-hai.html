Blood Pressure Kya Hai — Hypertension Ki Mukammal Urdu Guide

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • BP khoon ka pressure dhamniyon (arteries) ki diwaron pe
  • Normal: 120/80 mmHg
  • High BP (Hypertension): 130/80+ ya 140/90+
  • Pakistan mein 50% high BP cases undiagnosed
  • "Silent killer" — heart attack, stroke ka top cause

Blood Pressure Kya Hai?

Blood pressure (BP) woh force hai jo aap ka khoon dil dhamniyon (arteries) ki diwaron pe daalta hai jab heart pump karta hai. Yeh do numbers mein measure hota hai:

Two Numbers:

  • Systolic (upar wala): Heart contract karte waqt pressure (jab dil dhadakta hai)
  • Diastolic (neeche wala): Heart aaram karte waqt pressure (do dhadkans ke beech)

Example: 120/80 mmHg mein 120 systolic, 80 diastolic, mmHg = millimeters of mercury (measurement unit).

BP Categories (American Heart Association)
NormalBelow 120/80 mmHg
Elevated (Pre-hypertension)120-129 systolic, below 80 diastolic
Stage 1 Hypertension130-139 / 80-89
Stage 2 Hypertension140+ / 90+
Hypertensive Crisis180+ / 120+ — Emergency
Hypotension (Low BP)Below 90/60 mmHg

Pakistan Mein BP Crisis

  • 33% baligh population ki BP high hai
  • 50% un mein undiagnosed hain
  • Sirf 20% controlled hain (BP normal range mein)
  • Heart attack ka top single risk factor
  • Stroke ka most common cause

Why "Silent Killer"?

High BP ko silent killer kehte hain kyunke 95% logon ko koi symptoms nahi hote jab tak serious damage na ho jaye:

  • Heart attack
  • Stroke
  • Kidney failure
  • Eye damage (retinopathy)
  • Heart failure

Saalon tak silently damage karta rehta hai — isliye regular screening zaruri.

Hypertension Ki Types

1. Primary (Essential) Hypertension — 90% Cases

Specific cause identify nahi hota. Multiple factors:

  • Genetic
  • Lifestyle
  • Diet (namak zyada)
  • Stress
  • Age

2. Secondary Hypertension — 10% Cases

Specific underlying cause:

  • Kidney disease
  • Adrenal gland tumors
  • Thyroid problems
  • Sleep apnea
  • Birth control pills
  • Certain medications (steroids, NSAIDs)
  • Pregnancy (gestational hypertension)

Risk Factors

Modifiable (Aap Control Kar Sakte Hain):

  • Namak zyada — Pakistani diet 12+ grams (recommended 5)
  • Wazan zyada / motapa
  • Physical inactivity
  • Smoking aur paan/chaaliya
  • Stress
  • Alcohol
  • High cholesterol
  • Diabetes

Non-Modifiable:

  • Genetics / family history
  • Age (40+ risk barhta)
  • Race (South Asian higher risk)
  • Gender (men < 55 higher; women > 65 higher)

High BP Ki Symptoms (Aksar Koi Nahi)

Most logon mein koi symptoms nahi. Magar yeh ho sakte hain:

  • Sir dard (especially gardan ke peeche, subah)
  • Chakkar aana
  • Naak se khoon
  • Sans phoolna
  • Chest mein tanao
  • Ankhon mein blood spots
  • Thakawat
  • Confusion (severe cases)

Hypertensive Crisis (180/120+) Ke Symptoms:

  • Bohat shadeed sir dard
  • Chest pain
  • Sans bohat phoolna
  • Bain/dahini taraf weakness
  • Blur vision
  • Behoshi

Foran 1122 ya ER!

BP Sahi Tareeqe Se Measure Karein

Pre-Measurement:

  • 30 minute pehlay caffeine, smoking, exercise nahi
  • Pesab kar lein
  • 5 minute aaram karein
  • Sit quietly

During Measurement:

  • Sit in chair, peeth supported
  • Pair flat zameen pe
  • Baazu heart level pe (table pe)
  • Cuff nange baazu pe (kapde upar)
  • Cuff sahi size ka ho
  • Mat baat karein measurement ke dauran

Multiple Readings:

  • 2-3 readings, 1 minute apart
  • Average lein
  • Subah aur shaam, 1 hafte ke liye
  • Single high reading se diagnosis nahi

Cardiologist Se Mashwara

BP Ki Complications

Heart:

  • Heart attack
  • Heart failure
  • Atrial fibrillation
  • Coronary artery disease

Brain:

  • Stroke (#1 cause)
  • Dementia
  • Mini-strokes (TIA)

Kidneys:

  • Kidney damage
  • Kidney failure
  • Pakistan mein dialysis ka top cause

Eyes:

  • Retinopathy
  • Vision loss

Sexual Function:

  • Erectile dysfunction
  • Reduced libido

Low BP (Hypotension)

BP < 90/60 mmHg = low BP. Common in:

  • Athletes (often normal)
  • Pregnancy (mostly safe)
  • Dehydration
  • Severe blood loss
  • Heart problems
  • Endocrine issues (Addison's, hypothyroid)
  • Severe infections (sepsis)
  • Allergic reactions
  • Medications (BP dawai overdose)

Low BP Symptoms:

  • Chakkar aana, especially uthne pe
  • Behoshi
  • Nazar mein dhundlapan
  • Mitli
  • Thakawat
  • Concentration mein dushwari
  • Pasina aana

Hamla Khwateen Mein BP

Pregnancy mein BP changes:

  • 2nd trimester mein BP slightly drop ho sakti
  • 3rd trimester mein wapas normal
  • Pre-eclampsia: 140/90+ pregnancy mein — serious, foran doctor
  • Symptoms: sir dard, blur vision, hath/payon mein soojan, upper pet dard

BP Aur Pakistan

Pakistan mein high BP epidemic ki wajuhat:

  • Diet: Achaar, papar, namkeen biscuits, fast food (high sodium)
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Stress: Economic, family pressures
  • Smoking aur paan culture
  • Awareness kam
  • Genetic predisposition

Diagnosis Aur Monitoring

Initial Diagnosis:

Single reading enough nahi. 2-3 separate visits ya home monitoring 1 hafte:

  • Average BP > 130/80 = consider Stage 1 hypertension
  • Average BP > 140/90 = Stage 2
  • Lab tests: kidney function, electrolytes, lipid profile, blood sugar, urine

White Coat Hypertension:

Doctor ke clinic mein BP barh jati hai (anxiety) magar ghar pe normal. Home BP monitor use karein. Pakistan mein common.

Prevention

  • Namak 5 grams/day se kam
  • DASH diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension)
  • Roz 30 minute walk
  • Healthy weight
  • Smoking band
  • Stress management
  • Regular sleep
  • Annual BP check 18+ ke baad
  • Limit caffeine

Conclusion

Blood pressure aap ki health ka vital sign hai. High BP "silent killer" hai jo Pakistan mein 33% logon ko affect karti hai magar 50% ko pata nahi. Regular screening, lifestyle changes, aur jab zaruri ho dawai se completely manageable. Annual BP check, especially 30+ ke baad, life-saving ho sakti hai. Frequently Asked Questions

Normal blood pressure kya hota hai?

Normal BP 120/80 mmHg se neeche hai. 120-129/below 80 = elevated (pre-hypertension). 130-139/80-89 = Stage 1 hypertension. 140/90+ = Stage 2. 180/120+ = hypertensive crisis (emergency). Low BP < 90/60. Single reading se diagnosis nahi — multiple readings over 1 hafta required.

High BP ki symptoms kya hain?

95% logon mein koi symptoms nahi — isliye "silent killer". Kabhi kabhi: sir dard (especially gardan ke peeche, subah), chakkar, naak se khoon, sans phoolna, chest tanao, ankhon mein blood spots, thakawat. Hypertensive crisis (180/120+) mein: shadeed sir dard, chest pain, sans phoolna, weakness, blur vision, behoshi — foran ER. Annual screening zaruri hai 18+ ke baad.

BP test ka sahi tareeqa kya hai?

Pre-test: 30 minute pehlay caffeine, smoking, exercise nahi. Pesab kar lein. 5 minute aaram. During: Sit chair, peeth supported, pair flat, baazu heart level pe (table pe), cuff nange baazu pe (sahi size), don't talk. Multiple readings: 2-3 readings, 1 minute apart, average lein. Subah aur shaam, 1 hafte ke liye for accurate diagnosis.

High BP genetic hai?

Haan, genetic component strong hai. Direct relative (parent, sibling) ki high BP ho to aap ka risk doubled. Dono parents ho to risk significantly higher. South Asian ancestry mein baseline risk Europeans se 2x higher. Magar lifestyle factors (weight, namak, exercise, smoking, stress) genes ke saath interact karte hain — high-risk logon mein bhi prevention possible. Healthy lifestyle se 50%+ risk reduction.

Low BP khatarnak hai?

Low BP (90/60 se kam) zyadatar logon mein safe hai agar symptoms nahi. Athletes mein normal hai. Magar achanak symptoms (chakkar, behoshi, blur vision, mitli) ho to causes investigate karein: dehydration, blood loss, heart problems, endocrine issues, sepsis, ya BP dawai overdose. Pregnancy ke 2nd trimester mein normal slight drop. Severe symptoms ya behoshi mein ER zaruri.
