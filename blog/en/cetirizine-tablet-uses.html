Cetirizine Tablet Uses, Dosage and Side Effects in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Cetirizine is a second-generation antihistamine — effective for allergy, hives, and hay fever with minimal sedation
  • Standard adult dose: 10 mg once daily, preferably at night
  • Available in Pakistan as Zyrtec, Zirtec, Cetril, and affordable generics
  • Causes mild drowsiness in ~10% of people — much less than Piriton (chlorphenamine)
  • Safe for most adults; use with caution in kidney disease and during pregnancy

What is Cetirizine?

Cetirizine is a second-generation antihistamine used to relieve allergy symptoms. Chemically it is a piperazine derivative — the active metabolite of hydroxyzine — that works by selectively blocking peripheral H1 histamine receptors. Because it crosses the blood-brain barrier far less than older antihistamines, it causes significantly less sedation than first-generation drugs like chlorphenamine (Piriton) while still delivering powerful allergy relief.

In Pakistan, cetirizine is one of the most commonly dispensed allergy tablets at pharmacies, sold under brand names Zyrtec (GSK/UCB), Zirtec, and Cetril, as well as dozens of affordable generics. It is available over the counter at virtually every pharmacy across the country.

How Does Cetirizine Work?

When your body encounters an allergen — dust, pollen, pet dander, a food protein — immune cells release histamine. Histamine binds to H1 receptors in the skin, nose, eyes, and airways, triggering the familiar cascade of sneezing, itching, watery eyes, and runny nose.

Cetirizine competitively and selectively blocks H1 receptors, preventing histamine from binding. Importantly it acts mainly on peripheral H1 receptors (skin, nasal mucosa, airways) rather than central ones, which is why drowsiness is far less pronounced than with chlorphenamine. The drug is absorbed rapidly after oral dosing, reaches peak plasma concentration in about 1 hour, and maintains effective levels for 24 hours — making once-daily dosing sufficient.

What is Cetirizine Used For?

1. Allergic Rhinitis (Hay Fever, Dust Allergy, Seasonal Allergy)

This is the primary indication. Pakistan's cities — particularly Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad — experience severe dust, smog, and seasonal pollen burdens. Millions of Pakistanis suffer from perennial allergic rhinitis triggered by house dust mites, cockroach allergens, and animal dander, with additional seasonal peaks from grass and tree pollen in spring. Cetirizine reliably reduces sneezing, nasal congestion, runny nose, and post-nasal drip associated with these triggers. For best results, take it daily throughout the allergy season rather than only when symptoms flare.

2. Urticaria (Hives / Pitti)

Cetirizine is a first-line treatment for both acute and chronic urticaria. Urticaria — known locally as pitti — presents as raised, intensely itchy welts on the skin that may come and go. It can be triggered by foods (prawns, mango, nuts), drugs, latex, infections, or cold exposure. Cetirizine rapidly reduces the itch and helps the wheals resolve, often within a few hours. For chronic spontaneous urticaria lasting more than 6 weeks, daily cetirizine is the recommended maintenance therapy.

3. Allergic Conjunctivitis (Itchy, Red, Watery Eyes)

When histamine is released in the conjunctiva, the eyes become red, itchy, and watery. Cetirizine's systemic H1 blockade reduces conjunctival inflammation and is effective for allergic eye symptoms, often working alongside allergen avoidance. It is frequently combined with antihistamine eye drops for faster local relief.

4. Eczema / Atopic Dermatitis — Itch Relief

While cetirizine does not treat the underlying inflammation of eczema, it significantly reduces the itch component — particularly the nocturnal itch that disrupts sleep. Taking it at night helps patients sleep better and reduces the scratch-itch cycle that worsens skin barrier damage. It is used as an adjunct alongside emollients and topical steroids.

5. Insect Bite and Sting Reactions

Local allergic reactions to mosquito bites, bee stings, and ant bites — causing localised swelling, redness, and intense itching — respond well to cetirizine. It is particularly useful during summer months in Pakistan when mosquito-borne allergy reactions are common.

6. Food Allergy Symptoms (Mild)

For mild food allergy symptoms such as oral itching, skin rash, and hives after eating shellfish, mango, or other common Pakistani dietary triggers, cetirizine provides relief. It is not suitable for anaphylaxis (severe allergic reaction with throat swelling or breathing difficulty) — that requires injectable adrenaline (epinephrine) immediately.

When to take: For adults who experience mild drowsiness, taking cetirizine at night turns this side effect into an advantage — better sleep during allergy season. For chronic allergies (year-round dust allergy, perennial rhinitis), take it every day for sustained control rather than on-demand. For seasonal allergies, start a week before the season begins for optimal protection.

Cetirizine vs Other Antihistamines — Which Is Right for You?

Cetirizine vs Loratadine (Clarityn)

Both are second-generation antihistamines and are often compared directly. Loratadine is considered truly non-drowsy — it crosses the blood-brain barrier even less than cetirizine and is the better choice if you need to drive or operate machinery without any risk of sedation. Cetirizine, however, is slightly more potent for itch and hive relief and may work faster in some patients. For urticaria, most Pakistani dermatologists prefer cetirizine.

Cetirizine vs Levocetirizine (Xyzal)

Levocetirizine is the active R-enantiomer of cetirizine — essentially the pharmacologically active half of the cetirizine molecule. At 5 mg, levocetirizine delivers equivalent or slightly superior efficacy compared to cetirizine 10 mg, with marginally less sedation. It is available in Pakistan as Xyzal and generic levocetirizine but is more expensive. For most patients, generic cetirizine 10 mg offers excellent value.

Cetirizine vs Chlorphenamine (Piriton)

Chlorphenamine (Piriton) is a first-generation antihistamine that has been available in Pakistan for decades. It is significantly more sedating than cetirizine — many people feel drowsy or mentally "foggy" after taking it — and its effects last only 4–6 hours, requiring multiple daily doses. Cetirizine is preferred for daily use because of its once-daily dosing, milder sedation profile, and sustained allergy control. Piriton may still be useful as a short-term sedating antihistamine in specific situations (e.g., to help a child sleep through a very itchy night).

Side Effects of Cetirizine

Common Side Effects

  • Mild drowsiness — occurs in approximately 10% of users; much less than Piriton but more than loratadine
  • Dry mouth — drink plenty of water
  • Headache — usually mild and transient
  • Fatigue — especially in the first few days

Rare but Notable Side Effects

  • Urinary retention — difficulty passing urine; more common in men with prostate enlargement
  • Blurred vision
  • Palpitations (at high doses)
  • Paradoxical agitation — particularly in young children

Interaction with Alcohol

Alcohol significantly enhances cetirizine's sedative effect. Avoid alcohol completely on days you take cetirizine, especially if driving or working with machinery.

Who Should Avoid or Use Cetirizine Cautiously?

  • Severe kidney disease — cetirizine is renally excreted; dose reduction or avoidance required
  • Prostate enlargement — risk of urinary retention
  • Epilepsy — use with caution; monitor for increased seizure frequency
  • Pregnancy (first trimester and near delivery) — discuss with your doctor
  • Children under 2 years — not recommended
  • Liver disease — no dose adjustment needed as cetirizine is not extensively metabolised by the liver, but monitor carefully

Safety in Pregnancy and Breastfeeding

Cetirizine is classified as Pregnancy Category B. Animal reproduction studies showed no evidence of harm to the fetus, and human data are generally reassuring. However, as with all medicines in pregnancy, it should only be used when the benefit outweighs the risk. The second trimester is considered the safest window if treatment is needed. Avoid in the first trimester and in the weeks before delivery (may cause neonatal withdrawal symptoms). Loratadine is often preferred during pregnancy as it has slightly more safety data.

Cetirizine passes into breast milk in small amounts. The standard advice is to avoid antihistamines during breastfeeding if possible, or to use the lowest effective dose and observe the infant for drowsiness.

Drug Interactions

  • Alcohol — enhanced CNS depression; avoid
  • Other sedatives, sleeping tablets, benzodiazepines — additive sedation
  • Theophylline — may slightly reduce cetirizine clearance at high theophylline doses
  • Ritonavir (HIV medicine) — increases cetirizine plasma levels; reduce dose

Cetirizine has very few clinically significant drug interactions compared to many other medicines, which contributes to its wide safety profile.

Allergy in Pakistan — Why Cetirizine Is So Popular

Pakistan's urban environments present a high allergen burden year-round. Karachi's proximity to the sea and its massive population density create persistent house dust mite exposure. Lahore and Islamabad suffer extreme smog and particulate pollution in winter months, worsening existing respiratory allergies. Spring pollen from mulberry trees — a major allergen across Punjab — triggers allergic rhinitis epidemics annually. In this context, an effective, affordable, once-daily allergy tablet that does not heavily sedate is exactly what most Pakistani allergy sufferers need — and cetirizine fits that description well.

Generic cetirizine is available in Pakistan for as little as 30–50 PKR per tablet, making it one of the most cost-effective allergy treatments available. Brand-name Zyrtec is slightly more expensive but widely trusted.

Tips for Getting the Most from Cetirizine

  • Take at night to turn mild drowsiness into better sleep
  • Take it every day during allergy season — do not wait for symptoms to appear
  • Stay hydrated to counter dry mouth
  • Combine with nasal saline rinse (neti pot or saline spray) for better rhinitis control
  • For dust allergy: use dust-mite-proof pillow covers and wash bedding weekly in hot water
  • If drowsiness is a problem, try switching to loratadine or levocetirizine
  • If 10 mg is not fully controlling symptoms, consult a doctor — do not self-increase the dose

Conclusion

Cetirizine is one of Pakistan's most trusted and widely used allergy tablets — effective, affordable, and well-tolerated by most patients. Its once-daily dosing, superior efficacy over first-generation antihistamines like Piriton, and broad coverage of allergy symptoms from rhinitis and hives to itching and conjunctivitis make it the go-to choice for allergy sufferers across the country. Use it at night, take it consistently during allergy season, and consult a doctor if symptoms persist beyond two weeks or worsen. Frequently Asked Questions

What is cetirizine used for?

Cetirizine is a second-generation antihistamine used to treat allergic rhinitis (hay fever, dust allergy, seasonal allergy), urticaria (hives/pitti), allergic conjunctivitis (itchy red eyes), eczema itch, insect bite reactions, and mild food allergy symptoms. It is available in Pakistan as Zyrtec, Zirtec, Cetril, and affordable generics. It works within about 1 hour and lasts 24 hours.

What is the correct cetirizine dose for adults and children?

Adults and children 12 years and older: 10 mg once daily, preferably at night. Children 6–11 years: 5 mg twice daily or 10 mg once daily. Children 2–5 years: 2.5 mg twice daily (syrup). Elderly patients: start at 5 mg daily. For chronic allergies, take every day — not just when symptoms appear — for sustained control.

Does cetirizine cause drowsiness?

Cetirizine causes mild drowsiness in approximately 10% of people — far less than first-generation antihistamines like chlorphenamine (Piriton) but slightly more than loratadine. Taking it at night minimises the impact. Avoid alcohol as it significantly worsens sedation. Most people can drive and work normally while taking cetirizine.

What are the side effects of cetirizine?

Common side effects: mild drowsiness, dry mouth, headache, and fatigue — usually mild and often improve after a few days. Rare side effects include urinary retention (especially in men with prostate problems), blurred vision, and palpitations. Paradoxical agitation can occur in young children. Cetirizine should be used with caution in severe kidney disease.

Is cetirizine safe during pregnancy?

Cetirizine is Pregnancy Category B — generally considered relatively safe, but should only be taken when clearly needed. It is best avoided in the first trimester and near delivery. Loratadine is often preferred during pregnancy as the first-choice antihistamine. Always consult your doctor before taking any medicine while pregnant.
