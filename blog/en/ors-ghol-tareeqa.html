How to Mix ORS Solution — Dehydration and Diarrhea Guide

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • ORS restores the essential salt-sugar balance lost in diarrhea and dehydration
  • The correct ratio matters — mix exactly 1 sachet with 1 litre of clean water
  • Use the mixed solution within 24 hours, then discard it
  • Dehydration occurs quickly in children — start ORS immediately
  • Go to the hospital immediately if severe dehydration signs appear

What Is ORS?

ORS (Oral Rehydration Solution) is a simple but life-saving mixture containing a scientifically balanced ratio of salt and sugar — it quickly restores fluids and electrolytes lost from diarrhea or vomiting. It's available affordably at every pharmacy and medical store in Pakistan.

How to Correctly Prepare ORS Solution

  1. Take 1 litre of clean, boiled (and cooled) water — precise measurement matters
  2. Add the full contents of one ORS sachet to the water
  3. Mix thoroughly until the powder is completely dissolved
  4. Store in a clean, covered container

CRITICAL: Never use less or more than 1 litre of water — the wrong concentration can be harmful, especially in children.

When and How to Drink ORS

  • After every episode of diarrhea/vomiting — sip continuously
  • Drink sip by sip — don't gulp a large glass at once, especially if vomiting
  • Adults: 200-400ml after each loose motion
  • For children: weight-based amount as advised by a doctor

How Long Can Mixed ORS Be Used?

Use the mixed ORS solution within 24 hours — avoid keeping it longer even if refrigerated, as there's a contamination risk. Discard leftover solution after 24 hours and prepare a fresh batch.

Signs of Dehydration in Children

  • Dry mouth, no tears
  • Little to no urination (6+ hours)
  • Lethargy or irritability
  • Sunken eyes
  • Skin that's slow to bounce back when pinched

What Else to Do Alongside ORS

  • Continue normal feeding (continue breastfeeding for infants)
  • A zinc supplement for diarrhea, on a doctor's advice — reduces recovery time
  • Temporarily avoid fatty or spicy food

ORS in Hot Weather

In Pakistan's intense summer heat, ORS isn't just for diarrhea — it's also very useful for heat exhaustion and dehydration from excessive sweating. This is especially important for laborers and outdoor workers.

EMERGENCY — Go to the Hospital Immediately

  • A child can't keep any ORS down (repeated vomiting)
  • Severe dehydration signs — sunken eyes, no urine, extreme lethargy
  • Bloody diarrhea
  • Persistent diarrhea with high fever
  • The child is becoming unconscious or has seizures

Conclusion

ORS is a simple, affordable, and highly effective solution for preventing dehydration. The correct ratio and timing matter — 1 sachet per 1 litre of water, used within 24 hours. Don't delay if you see signs of severe dehydration — consult a doctor on Ilaaj AI immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the correct ratio for mixing ORS?

Mix each sachet with exactly 1 litre of clean, boiled and cooled water. Never use less or more water — the wrong concentration can be harmful, especially risking sodium imbalance in children.

How long can mixed ORS be used?

Use it within 24 hours, even if refrigerated. Discard leftover solution after 24 hours and prepare fresh to avoid contamination.

Can ORS be given during vomiting?

Yes, but give it sip by sip — don't give a large amount at once, as it can trigger more vomiting. Give small sips every 5-10 minutes, gradually increasing the amount.

What are the signs of dehydration in children?

Dry mouth, no tears, little to no urination, lethargy or irritability, and sunken eyes are signs of dehydration. Start ORS as soon as you see these signs, and see a doctor if they're severe.

Does ORS treat diarrhea?

No, ORS doesn't stop diarrhea — it only replaces fluid and electrolyte loss to prevent dehydration. The underlying cause of diarrhea (infection, food poisoning) should be discussed with a doctor separately if it persists.
