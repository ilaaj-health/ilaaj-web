Sore Throat Treatment — Causes, Home Remedies and Best Medicine

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • 90% of sore throats are viral — antibiotics are useless
  • Strep throat (bacterial) requires antibiotics
  • Warm salt water gargle is the most proven home remedy
  • Severe difficulty swallowing or pus is an emergency
  • Fever plus sore throat in children requires a test

Causes of Sore Throat

Sore throat is very common in Pakistan during season changes and smog season. Causes include:

Sore Throat — Causes
Viral (90%)Common cold, flu, COVID
Bacterial (10%)Strep throat — antibiotics required
AllergySmog, dust, pollen
Acid refluxStomach acid reaching the throat
Smoke / PollutionSmoking or passive smoke
Vocal strainOveruse of the voice

Recognising the Symptoms

  • Pain or irritation in the throat
  • Difficulty swallowing
  • Hoarse or changed voice
  • Fever (more prominent in bacterial infection)
  • Swollen and red tonsils
  • White or yellow patches on tonsils (strep)
  • Headache, body aches

Viral vs Bacterial Sore Throat

Viral (Antibiotics Useless):

  • Cough, runny nose, blocked nose
  • Mild to moderate fever
  • Hoarse voice
  • Watery eyes
  • Resolves on its own within 3–7 days

Strep (Bacterial — Antibiotics Required):

  • Sudden severe throat pain
  • Fever above 101°F
  • White pus patches on the tonsils
  • No cough
  • Swollen glands in the neck (lymph nodes)
  • Headache, stomach pain (especially in children)

Confirmation: Via throat swab test or rapid strep test. A doctor can also make a good assessment based on symptoms alone.

Home Remedies for Sore Throat

1. Warm Salt Water Gargle

The most proven home remedy. Mix 1/2 teaspoon of salt in 1 cup of warm water. Gargle for 30–60 seconds, 3–4 times a day.

2. Honey and Ginger

Mix 1 teaspoon of honey, lemon juice, and grated ginger in warm water. Take 2–3 times a day.

3. Warm Broth / Soup

Chicken broth soothes the throat and keeps you hydrated.

4. Hot or Cold Beverages

Warm green tea or cold ice cream — both help (go with your preference).

5. Steam Inhalation

10 minutes, twice a day. Drape a towel over your head to trap the steam.

6. Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water — keeps the throat moist.

7. Humidifier or Damp Cloth

Maintain humidity in the room, especially when using air conditioning.

Best OTC Medicine

  • Lozenges: Strepsils, Vicks Throat Drops, Difflam — instant soothing
  • Anti-inflammatory: Brufen 400 mg or Panadol 500 mg — for pain and fever
  • Throat sprays: Difflam spray (benzydamine) — strong relief
  • Antihistamine: For allergy-related sore throat — Telfast, Zyrtec
  • Antacid: If caused by GERD — Risek 20 mg or Pantop

Antibiotics for Strep Throat

If strep throat is confirmed:

  • Penicillin V (Calvepen): First line, 10-day course
  • Amoxicillin (Amoxil): 7–10 days
  • Augmentin 625 mg: For resistant cases
  • Erythromycin / Azithromycin: For penicillin allergy

Important: Complete the full antibiotic course. Stopping early risks rheumatic fever (heart valve damage). In Pakistan, untreated strep is a major cause of rheumatic heart disease — correct diagnosis is essential.

Tonsillitis

If tonsils become recurrently infected (5–7 times a year), a tonsillectomy (surgical removal of tonsils) may be required. Evaluation by an ENT specialist is needed.

Special Considerations in Pakistan

  • Smog season: N95 mask and indoor air purifier
  • Fasting (Roza): Pay special attention to hydration at sehri and iftar
  • Singers and teachers: Voice rest and warm-up exercises
  • Acid reflux: Late dinners and spicy food are common triggers in Pakistan

Preventing Sore Throat

  • Wash your hands regularly
  • Keep distance from sick people
  • Avoid smoking and passive smoke
  • Wear an N95 mask in smog
  • Stay hydrated
  • Rest your voice when your throat is sore
  • Avoid direct airflow from air conditioning
  • Change your toothbrush every 3 months

When to See a Doctor Urgently

  • Difficulty breathing or swallowing
  • Difficulty opening the mouth (jaw locked)
  • Muffled voice (possible peritonsillar abscess)
  • Fever above 101°F with pus on the tonsils
  • Coughing up blood
  • Severe pain lasting more than 3 days
  • Any sore throat lasting more than 1 week
  • Rash on the body (scarlet fever)

Conclusion

Most sore throats are viral and resolve on their own within 5–7 days. Antibiotics are only needed for strep throat. In Pakistan, untreated strep causes rheumatic heart disease — so correct diagnosis is crucial. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best treatment for a sore throat?

The most proven approach: warm salt water gargle 3–4 times a day, warm fluids (tea, soup, honey and ginger), and Strepsils or Difflam lozenges. For pain, Brufen 400 mg or Panadol 500 mg. 90% of sore throats are viral and resolve on their own within 5–7 days. Antibiotics are only needed for strep throat — confirmation from a doctor is essential.

Are antibiotics necessary for a sore throat?

Only for bacterial infection (strep throat). Signs: sudden severe pain, fever above 101°F, pus patches on the tonsils, no cough, swollen glands in the neck. Antibiotics are useless for viral sore throat and cause side effects. A doctor confirms with a throat swab or rapid strep test. If strep is confirmed, a 10-day course of Calvepen or Amoxil is required.

Why does the voice become hoarse?

Common causes of a hoarse voice: viral infection (laryngitis), overuse of the voice, smoking, acid reflux, allergies, or in rare cases vocal cord nodules. It should resolve within a week. Voice rest, hydration, and quitting smoking are the most important steps. If hoarseness persists for more than 3 weeks, see an ENT specialist — a vocal cord examination is needed.

What is the treatment for a sore throat in children?

In children, a combination of fever and sore throat may indicate strep — a doctor is needed. Calpol is safe for fever and pain. For children aged 6 and above, salt water gargling, lozenges, and warm honey (for children over 1 year) are safe. Give soft foods (broth, yoghurt, ice cream) and plenty of fluids. If there are pus patches or swallowing is very difficult, see a doctor immediately.

What should I do about swollen tonsils?

First try salt water gargling, lozenges, and painkillers. If there are white pus patches on the tonsils, fever, or difficulty breathing, it is likely a bacterial infection and antibiotics are needed. For recurrent tonsillitis (5–7 times a year), a tonsillectomy may be required. See an ENT specialist for evaluation. Both hot and cold beverages can be soothing — go with your preference.
