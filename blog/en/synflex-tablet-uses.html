Synflex Tablet Uses, Dosage and Side Effects in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Synflex = naproxen sodium, a fast-acting NSAID painkiller
  • Lasts 8–12 hours — longer than Brufen (6 hours)
  • Used for joint pain, period cramps, muscle pain, dental pain
  • Take with food — protects the stomach
  • Avoid in kidney disease, peptic ulcer, and third trimester of pregnancy

What Is Synflex?

Synflex is the brand name for naproxen sodium — the sodium salt of naproxen that absorbs faster than plain naproxen. It is an NSAID (non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug) that blocks prostaglandins, the chemicals that cause pain and inflammation. Unlike Brufen (ibuprofen), which lasts 6 hours, Synflex provides 8–12 hours of relief, so two doses daily are usually enough.

Synflex Tablet Uses

1. Musculoskeletal Pain and Muscle Injuries

Sports injuries, sprains, strains, and overuse injuries.

2. Arthritis and Joint Pain

Osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and gout flares.

3. Menstrual Cramps (Dysmenorrhea)

Very effective for period pain — reduces prostaglandins that cause cramps. Start 1–2 days before expected onset for best results.

4. Dental and Toothache Pain

Used by dentists post-procedure and for toothache relief.

5. Back Pain and Neck Pain

Acute and chronic musculoskeletal pain in the spine and neck.

6. Headache and Migraine

Can relieve both tension headache and migraine headache.

7. Fever (Secondary)

Has an antipyretic effect, though paracetamol is preferred for fever alone.

8. Post-Surgical Pain

Used after minor procedures for pain management.

Side Effects

Common:

  • Stomach upset, nausea, heartburn
  • Headache, dizziness
  • Fluid retention (mild ankle swelling)
  • Drowsiness

Serious:

  • GI bleeding / peptic ulcer — the main risk with long-term use
  • Kidney damage — at high doses or with dehydration
  • Cardiovascular risk — increased heart attack/stroke risk with chronic use
  • Allergic reaction — rash, swelling, difficulty breathing
  • Liver toxicity — rare

Warning Signs — Stop and See a Doctor

  • Black/tarry stools or blood in vomit (GI bleed)
  • Sudden severe chest pain (cardiac event)
  • Swelling of face/lips/throat (allergy)
  • Significant reduction in urine output (kidney damage)
  • Yellowing of eyes/skin (liver)

Who Should NOT Take Synflex

  • Active peptic ulcer or GI bleeding
  • Severe kidney disease (GFR < 30)
  • Severe liver disease
  • Heart failure
  • Third trimester of pregnancy (closes fetal ductus arteriosus)
  • Aspirin-sensitive asthma (may cross-react)
  • Allergy to any NSAID
  • Children under 12 years

Stomach Protection

Adding a PPI (Risek 20, Pantop) is recommended when:

  • Age over 60
  • Taking for more than 7 days
  • Taking blood thinners (aspirin, warfarin) simultaneously
  • Previous history of peptic ulcer
  • Concurrent steroid use

Drug Interactions

  • Aspirin / Loprin: Avoid combining — doubles GI bleeding risk and reduces each other's effectiveness
  • Warfarin: Increased bleeding risk — INR monitoring needed
  • Lithium: Naproxen increases lithium levels — toxicity risk
  • ACE inhibitors / ARBs: Reduced blood pressure control, kidney risk
  • Diuretics: Reduced diuretic effect
  • Methotrexate: Toxicity risk
  • SSRIs: Increased GI bleeding risk

Use in Special Situations

  • Pregnancy: Safe in first and second trimester (short-term only); strictly avoid in third trimester
  • Breastfeeding: Avoid; passes into breast milk
  • Elderly (60+): Use lowest effective dose + PPI; kidneys are more vulnerable
  • Dengue patients: NEVER use NSAIDs in dengue — risk of haemorrhage; use paracetamol only

Pakistan Brands of Naproxen Sodium

  • Synflex 275 mg / 550 mg (Sami Pharma)
  • Atrosan 275 mg / 550 mg
  • Naprosyn (plain naproxen, not sodium salt — slightly slower)
  • Generic naproxen sodium

Conclusion

Synflex (naproxen sodium) is one of Pakistan's most reliable painkillers — longer-acting than Brufen and effective for joint pain, period cramps, and musculoskeletal injuries. However, it carries real GI, kidney, and cardiac risks, especially with long-term use. Always take with food, avoid in kidney disease and peptic ulcer, and add a PPI if taking for more than a week. Frequently Asked Questions

What is Synflex 275 used for?

Synflex 275 mg is used for mild to moderate pain including joint pain (arthritis, gout), muscle pain, period cramps, dental pain, back pain, and headaches. It is an NSAID that works by reducing prostaglandins responsible for pain and inflammation. The 275 mg strength is available over the counter in Pakistan.

How is Synflex different from Brufen?

Both are NSAIDs but Synflex (naproxen sodium) lasts 8–12 hours while Brufen (ibuprofen) lasts 4–6 hours. This means Synflex is taken twice daily while Brufen requires 3 doses. Synflex is often preferred for period pain and arthritis because of its longer duration. However, Synflex's longer presence in the body means slightly more GI exposure with each day of use.

Can I take Synflex on an empty stomach?

No — always take with food, milk, or an antacid. NSAIDs irritate the stomach lining and can cause ulcers if taken on an empty stomach. If you experience heartburn or black stools, stop immediately and consult a doctor.

Is Synflex safe during pregnancy?

Synflex is generally avoided in pregnancy. In the first and second trimester, short-term use may be considered only under medical supervision. In the third trimester (after 28 weeks), Synflex is strictly contraindicated — it can prematurely close a fetal blood vessel (ductus arteriosus). Paracetamol is the safest painkiller in pregnancy.

Can Synflex be taken in dengue?

No — never take Synflex (or any NSAID) during dengue fever. NSAIDs increase the risk of internal bleeding in dengue patients because of the thrombocytopenia (low platelets). Use paracetamol only for fever in dengue.
