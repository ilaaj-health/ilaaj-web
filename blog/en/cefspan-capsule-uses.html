Cefspan Uses — Complete Cefixime Treatment Guide

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Cefspan contains cefixime — a third-generation cephalosporin antibiotic
  • Used for UTI, respiratory, and typhoid infections
  • A syrup form is also available for children
  • Completing the full course is essential, even if symptoms improve
  • Use with caution in patients with penicillin allergy

What Is Cefspan?

Cefspan contains the active ingredient cefixime — a third-generation cephalosporin antibiotic effective against a broad spectrum of bacteria. It's available in Pakistan as both capsules and a children's syrup.

What Is Cefspan Used For?

  • Urinary tract infections (UTI)
  • Respiratory infections — bronchitis, pharyngitis, tonsillitis
  • Typhoid fever — a first-line option in some cases
  • Ear infections (otitis media) — common in children
  • Gonorrhea — in some cases
  • Skin infections

Dosage — Adults

  • Standard dose: 200mg twice daily, or 400mg once daily
  • For typhoid: Higher dose per doctor's guidance, for 10-14 days
  • Can be taken with or without food

Dosage — For Children (Syrup)

Weight-based dosing, prescribed by a doctor — approximately 8mg/kg per day, divided into one or two doses. Shake the syrup well and use the exact measuring cup/spoon to avoid overdosing.

CRITICAL: Complete the Full Course

Even if the child or adult feels better within 2-3 days, completing the full prescribed course is essential. Stopping midway can allow bacteria to become resistant and cause the infection to return.

Side Effects

Common:

  • Stomach upset, diarrhea
  • Nausea
  • Stomach pain
  • Headache

Serious (rare):

  • Severe allergic reaction — especially cross-reactivity risk in penicillin-allergic patients
  • Severe diarrhea (C. difficile colitis)
  • Liver function changes

Caution with Penicillin Allergy

Cefspan belongs to the cephalosporin class, which is related to penicillin. If there's a history of severe penicillin allergy (anaphylaxis), Cefspan may also carry a cross-reaction risk — always inform your doctor.

Who Should Be Cautious

  • History of penicillin allergy
  • Kidney disease patients (dose adjustment needed)
  • Pregnancy — use only with a doctor's advice

When to See a Doctor

  • Symptoms don't improve after 3 days
  • Rash, swelling, or breathing difficulty (allergic reaction)
  • Severe diarrhea
  • A child becomes lethargic along with fever

Conclusion

Cefspan (cefixime) is a versatile antibiotic used for various infections in both adults and children. Completing the full course and following your doctor's guidance is essential. Discuss your own or your child's exact needs with a doctor on Ilaaj AI.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is Cefspan given to children?

It's given in syrup form for children, with weight-based dosing (approximately 8mg/kg per day). The doctor calculates the exact dose based on the child's weight and infection type — don't self-dose.

What's the risk of Cefspan with penicillin allergy?

Cefspan belongs to the cephalosporin class, which is chemically related to penicillin. Those with a history of severe penicillin allergy (anaphylaxis) may have a cross-reaction risk — always tell your doctor about your allergy history.

What is Cefspan best used for?

It's commonly prescribed for UTIs, respiratory infections, typhoid fever, and ear infections in children. It should only be used after a doctor's diagnosis — don't self-select antibiotics.

Can I stop Cefspan midway if I feel better?

No, even if symptoms improve within 2-3 days, completing the full prescribed course (usually 7-10 days) is essential to fully eliminate bacteria and prevent resistance.

Can I use expired Cefspan syrup?

Absolutely not — reconstituted syrup (mixed with water) is usually only effective for 14 days when refrigerated, and expires after that. Always check the date and discard expired syrup.
