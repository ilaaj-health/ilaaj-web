Panadol — Uses, Dosage, Side Effects and Price in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Panadol = paracetamol = acetaminophen — all the same drug
  • Adult dose: 500–1000 mg every 4–6 hours
  • Maximum 4,000 mg (4 g) per 24 hours — overdose causes liver damage
  • Safe in pregnancy (unlike Brufen)
  • For children, use Calpol syrup — dose based on weight

What Is Panadol?

Panadol is GlaxoSmithKline's brand name for paracetamol (internationally known as acetaminophen). It is Pakistan's most common over-the-counter painkiller and antipyretic (fever-reducing medicine). Millions of Panadol tablets are sold across Pakistan every day.

What Is Panadol Used For?

1. Fever

Panadol is the safest and most effective medicine to bring down fever. It can be used for viral, bacterial, and dengue fever. In dengue, use only Panadol — never Brufen or Aspirin.

2. Headache

Effective for tension headaches and migraines. It is the first-choice medicine for mild-to-moderate pain.

3. Body Pain

  • Muscle aches
  • Joint pain (osteoarthritis)
  • Back pain
  • Period cramps (can alternate with Brufen)

4. Toothache

Effective for mild to moderate dental pain.

5. Post-Surgery Pain

Taken at regular intervals as directed by a doctor.

6. Cold and Flu Symptoms

Useful for body aches, headache, and mild fever associated with colds and flu.

Different Forms of Panadol

1. Panadol 500 mg (Standard)

The most common form. Used for fever and mild pain.

2. Panadol 1000 mg

Stronger single dose, taken less frequently.

3. Panadol Extra (500 mg + 65 mg caffeine)

More effective for migraines and tension headaches. Caffeine enhances the painkilling effect.

4. Panadol Cold & Flu

Paracetamol combined with a decongestant and cough suppressant.

5. Calpol Syrup (for Children)

Available in different concentrations: Calpol Pediatric (120 mg/5 ml) and Calpol 6+ (250 mg/5 ml).

Panadol Side Effects

Panadol is very safe at the recommended dose. However:

Common (Mild):

  • Nausea or stomach upset (occasional)
  • Allergic skin rash (rare)

Serious (from overdose):

  • Liver damage / failure — the greatest danger
  • Kidney problems
  • Anaemia (with long-term high doses)
  • Severe allergic reaction (Stevens-Johnson syndrome — very rare)

Pregnancy and Breastfeeding:

Safe in pregnancy — it is the first-line painkiller. Also safe while breastfeeding.

Panadol Overdose — The Biggest Risk

Paracetamol overdose is a common method used in suicide attempts in Pakistan. 10 grams (20 tablets of 500 mg) can cause liver failure.

Symptoms (within 24–72 hours):

  • Nausea, vomiting
  • Pain in the upper abdomen (liver area)
  • Sweating
  • Yellow skin or eyes (jaundice — a late sign)
  • Confusion

Action:

  1. Go immediately to 1122 or the nearest emergency room
  2. The antidote (N-acetylcysteine) is effective if given within 8 hours
  3. Go to the ER even if you have no symptoms — silent damage can occur

Drug Interactions

Take care with the following medicines and substances:

  • Alcohol: Greatly increases the risk of liver damage
  • Warfarin (Coumadin): Increases bleeding risk
  • Anti-TB drugs (INH, Rifampin): Liver toxicity
  • Phenytoin, Carbamazepine: Reduce Panadol's effect

Special Populations

Liver Disease:

Halve the dose in hepatitis or cirrhosis; doctor consultation is essential.

Kidney Disease:

Adjust the dose in severe kidney impairment.

Elderly (65+):

A maximum of 3 grams (6 tablets) per day is safer.

Malnourished Patients:

Lower glutathione levels increase sensitivity — use a lower dose.

When to See a Doctor

  • You have been using Panadol for more than 3 days without relief
  • Fever is 102°F or above and has lasted more than 3 days
  • Pain is worsening despite the medicine
  • Yellow skin or eyes
  • Allergic reaction (rash, swelling)
  • A child has had a fever for more than 3 days
  • You are pregnant or breastfeeding and need ongoing pain relief

Conclusion

Panadol is Pakistan's safest and most widely used painkiller when taken at the recommended dose. For adults: 500–1000 mg every 4–6 hours, maximum 4 grams per day. For children, use Calpol syrup dosed by weight. Overdose can cause liver failure — do not exceed 4 grams in 24 hours. Frequently Asked Questions

How many Panadol tablets can I take in one day?

For adults and children aged 12 and over, the maximum is 4,000 mg (4 grams) per 24 hours — that is 8 tablets of 500 mg or 4 tablets of 1000 mg. Each single dose is 500–1000 mg every 4–6 hours. Exceeding this amount can cause liver damage or failure. For those aged 65 and over, a maximum of 3 grams (6 tablets) per day is safer.

Can Panadol be taken on an empty stomach?

Yes, Panadol (paracetamol) is completely safe on an empty stomach — unlike Brufen and aspirin. It does not cause stomach ulcers. However, if it upsets your stomach, you may take it with food. Do not take it without a doctor's advice if you have dengue, jaundice, or liver problems.

What is the correct Panadol dose for children?

Give children Calpol syrup — the dose depends on weight (15 mg/kg every 4–6 hours). Ages 3–6 months (4–7 kg): 2.5 ml. Ages 6–24 months (7–12 kg): 5 ml. Ages 2–4 years (12–16 kg): 7.5 ml. Ages 4–6 years: 10 ml. For babies under 3 months, any fever is a medical emergency — see a doctor immediately. Never give aspirin to children.

Is Panadol safe during pregnancy?

Yes, paracetamol (Panadol) is the safest painkiller in pregnancy and is the first-line option. It can be used when necessary for fever and pain. Brufen, Voltral, and Aspirin should be completely avoided in the third trimester. Panadol is also safe while breastfeeding. Consult a gynaecologist before taking any medication long-term.

What is the difference between Panadol Extra and regular Panadol?

Panadol Extra contains paracetamol 500 mg plus caffeine 65 mg. The caffeine boosts the painkilling effect by up to 40%, making it more effective for migraines and tension headaches. However, people sensitive to caffeine may experience restlessness or sleep difficulties. Avoid it at night. Consult a doctor if you have hypertension or heart problems. Regular Panadol is better for general use.
