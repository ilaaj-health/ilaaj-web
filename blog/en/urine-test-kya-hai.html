Urine Test (Urine R/E) Explained — Complete Screening Guide

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Urine R/E screens for UTI, kidney problems, and diabetes
  • It's an affordable, fast, and non-invasive test
  • A clean-catch midstream sample gives the most accurate result
  • Protein or blood in urine can indicate a serious underlying condition
  • Also commonly ordered during regular checkups

What Is a Urine Test (Urine R/E)?

Urine Routine Examination (Urine R/E) is a basic yet informative test that checks the physical, chemical, and microscopic properties of urine. It's helpful for early detection of UTIs, kidney problems, diabetes, and other conditions.

What Does a Urine Test Check?

  • Color and Clarity — an unusual color can indicate infection or dehydration
  • Protein — an indicator of kidney function; high protein signals possible kidney disease
  • Glucose (sugar) — can indicate undiagnosed diabetes
  • Blood (RBC) — a signal of UTI, kidney stones, or other conditions
  • WBC and Bacteria — confirms infection (UTI)
  • pH level — acidity/alkalinity balance
  • Specific Gravity — hydration status
  • Ketones — an indicator of uncontrolled diabetes or starvation

When Is a Urine Test Ordered?

  • UTI symptoms — burning during urination, frequent urination, foul smell
  • Routine health checkup
  • Pregnancy checkups — part of regular monitoring
  • Diabetes screening or monitoring
  • Suspected kidney disease
  • Abdominal or back pain (suspected kidney stones)
  • Pre-surgery evaluation

How to Give the Sample — Clean-Catch Method

  1. Wash your hands thoroughly
  2. Clean the genital area with the provided wipe
  3. Urinate a little into the toilet first (to remove contaminants)
  4. Collect the rest in the provided sterile cup
  5. Close the cup tightly and hand it to the lab immediately

This method is essential for an accurate result — contamination can give false results.

What Abnormal Results Mean

  • Protein present — kidney disease, high BP, or sometimes normal after exercise
  • Glucose present — an indication of diabetes
  • Blood present — UTI, kidney stones, or kidney disease
  • WBC/Bacteria present — confirms a UTI
  • Ketones present — uncontrolled diabetes, risk of DKA (diabetic ketoacidosis)

Preparing for the Test

No fasting is required — it can be tested at any time. Avoid drinking excessive water before the test — overly diluted urine can affect the result.

When to See a Doctor

  • Burning, frequent urination, or urgency
  • Unusual urine color (dark, red, cloudy)
  • Back or abdominal pain with urinary problems
  • Urinary difficulty accompanied by fever
  • Any abnormal value on the test

Conclusion

Frequently Asked Questions

Is fasting required for a urine test?

No, a urine test can be done at any time without fasting. Just avoid drinking excessive water before the test since overly diluted urine can affect the result.

Why is a clean-catch sample necessary?

A contaminated sample (without proper cleaning) can give false results — such as contamination showing WBC or bacteria present that isn't actually an infection. Following instructions matters for an accurate result.

What does protein in urine indicate?

Persistent protein can signal kidney disease or high blood pressure. Occasional mild protein can also occur from normal exercise or dehydration — a doctor may recommend further tests to confirm.

How does a urine test diagnose a UTI?

The presence of WBC (white blood cells) and bacteria in a urine test confirms a UTI. Combined with symptoms (burning, frequent urination), these findings help the doctor decide on diagnosis and antibiotic treatment.

Can a urine test detect diabetes?

Yes, the presence of glucose or ketones in urine can indicate undiagnosed or uncontrolled diabetes. To confirm, the doctor will also order blood sugar tests (fasting sugar, HbA1c).
