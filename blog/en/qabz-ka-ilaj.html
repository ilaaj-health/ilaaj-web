Constipation Treatment — Causes, Diet and Best Medicine Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Fewer than 3 bowel movements per week = constipation
  • 25–30 grams of fibre per day and 8 glasses of water are the most important steps
  • Isabgol (psyllium) is the safest natural laxative
  • 30 minutes of walking daily helps regulate bowel movements
  • Blood in stool, weight loss or abdominal pain = see a doctor urgently

What Is Constipation?

Constipation occurs when you have fewer than 3 bowel movements per week, or when stools are hard and difficult to pass. It is very common in Pakistan — a low-fibre diet (white bread, white rice), low water intake and lack of exercise are the biggest causes.

Symptoms of Constipation
Bowel movements per weekFewer than 3 = constipation
Stool qualityDry, hard, lumpy
Effort requiredStraining significantly
SensationFeeling bloated, incomplete emptying
Prevalence in Pakistan15–20% of adults — more common in women

Causes of Constipation

1. Diet (The Biggest Factor)

  • Low fibre: White bread, white rice, fast food
  • Insufficient water: Fewer than 8 glasses a day
  • Not enough fruit and vegetables
  • Excess dairy (in some individuals)

2. Lifestyle

  • Sedentary — no exercise
  • Ignoring the urge to go
  • Travel — change in routine
  • Spending too long on the phone in the bathroom

3. Medicines

  • Iron supplements
  • Calcium tablets
  • Painkillers (codeine, tramadol, morphine)
  • Anti-depressants (TCAs)
  • Blood pressure medicines (calcium channel blockers)
  • Antacids (aluminium-based)

4. Medical Conditions

  • Hypothyroidism
  • Diabetes
  • IBS-C (constipation-predominant irritable bowel syndrome)
  • Pregnancy
  • Parkinson's disease, multiple sclerosis
  • Colon cancer (rare but serious)

Home Remedies for Constipation

1. Warm Water on an Empty Stomach

2 glasses of warm water with lemon juice first thing in the morning. Stimulates bowel movement.

2. Isabgol (Psyllium Husk)

The safest natural fibre available. Soak 1–2 teaspoons in water overnight and drink in the morning, or mix into milk. Available from Hamdard and Qarshi.

3. Fruit and Vegetables

  • High-fibre fruit: Figs (anjeer), dates, prunes, papaya, guava
  • Vegetables: Spinach, fenugreek leaves (methi), broccoli, beans, black-eyed peas (lobia)
  • Other fruit: Apple (with skin), pear, ripe bananas

4. Whole Grains

Whole-wheat roti (not white flour), oats, brown rice. In Pakistan, the word "atta" is sometimes used as a marketing term — check for actual whole wheat on the label.

5. Probiotic Yoghurt

One cup of yoghurt daily improves gut health. Fortified probiotic yoghurts (Yoplait, Activia) are especially effective.

6. Olive Oil

1 teaspoon of olive oil on an empty stomach — acts as a natural lubricant.

7. Coffee

Caffeine stimulates bowel motility. One cup in the morning is helpful.

8. Nuts and Seeds

Walnuts, almonds, flaxseed (alsi), chia seeds — rich in fibre and omega-3.

Best OTC Laxatives

Try dietary changes first. If there is no improvement after 1–2 weeks:

Bulk-Forming (Safest):

  • Isabgol (psyllium): Hamdard, Qarshi, Naturalax
  • Methylcellulose

Osmotic:

  • Lactulose syrup (Duphalac): Safe and gentle
  • Magnesium hydroxide (Milk of Magnesia)
  • PEG (Macrogol, Forlax): Effective for severe constipation

Stimulant (Short-Term Only):

  • Bisacodyl (Dulcolax): Effective but can cause dependency
  • Senna: Natural but not for daily use

Stool Softeners:

  • Docusate: For hard, dry stools

Avoid Long-Term Stimulant Laxatives: Senna and Dulcolax cause the colon to become "lazy" with prolonged use.

Constipation During Pregnancy

Pregnancy hormones and iron supplements both worsen constipation. Safe options during pregnancy:

  • Plenty of fibre and water
  • Walking
  • Isabgol
  • Lactulose syrup (safe in pregnancy)
  • Avoid stimulant laxatives without doctor approval

Constipation in Children

  • Too much milk or early introduction of cow's milk
  • Stress during toilet training
  • Very low-fibre diet

Treatment:

  • Fruit (papaya, prune juice for 6+ months)
  • Plenty of water
  • Reduce cow's milk
  • Lactulose syrup (with doctor guidance)
  • Gentle clockwise tummy massage
  • Encouragement, not scolding during toilet training

Haemorrhoids (Piles) and Constipation

Long-term constipation is a leading cause of haemorrhoids. Symptoms include blood in the stool, anal pain and swelling. Treat the constipation first — increase fibre, drink more water. Sitz baths (sitting in warm water for 15 minutes) provide relief. Severe cases require a surgeon.

Special Considerations for Pakistan

  • Ramadan fasting: Dates at iftar act as a natural laxative; eat fibre-rich foods at sehri
  • Travel: Try to maintain your usual routine
  • Office workers: Take short walks every hour; keep high-fibre snacks at your desk
  • Older adults: Constipation is common with reduced mobility — a daily Isabgol routine helps

When to See a Doctor Urgently

  • Blood in the stool
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Severe, prolonged abdominal pain
  • Sudden change in stool shape (pencil-thin)
  • New-onset constipation in anyone over 50
  • Family history of colon cancer
  • Anaemia (fatigue, paleness)
  • Vomiting and abdominal bloating together
  • No bowel movement for more than 2 weeks
  • Severe abdominal distension in children

Long-Term Healthy Habits

  • 25–30 grams of fibre daily
  • 8+ glasses of water
  • 30 minutes of walking daily
  • Do not ignore the urge to go
  • Spend no more than 5 minutes in the bathroom
  • The squatting position is better for bowel movements (use a footstool)
  • No phone in the bathroom
  • Manage stress

Conclusion

The long-term solution for constipation lies in diet and lifestyle — laxatives are only a short-term tool. Isabgol, a fibre-rich diet and regular exercise resolve the majority of cases. Blood in the stool, unexplained weight loss or new-onset constipation in someone over 50 all require a doctor's assessment. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the quickest relief for constipation?

On an empty stomach in the morning: 2 glasses of warm water with lemon, 1–2 teaspoons of Isabgol (psyllium), fruit (papaya, figs, bananas) and a 30-minute walk. For stronger relief, Lactulose syrup (Duphalac) or a Dulcolax tablet at night. Do not use stimulant laxatives long-term. If there is no improvement within 24–48 hours or you have abdominal pain, see a doctor.

What are the best home remedies for constipation?

Isabgol (psyllium husk) is Pakistan's time-tested natural fibre — soak overnight in water and drink in the morning. Soak figs overnight and eat them in the morning. Dates, prune juice, papaya and a teaspoon of ghee with jaggery are all effective. On an empty stomach: 2 glasses of warm water and 1 teaspoon of olive oil. Daily yoghurt provides probiotics.

What is safe treatment for constipation during pregnancy?

Safe options during pregnancy: plenty of fibre (fruit, vegetables, whole grains), 10+ glasses of water daily, walking, Isabgol and Lactulose syrup (Duphalac). Iron supplements worsen constipation — discuss with your doctor. Avoid stimulant laxatives (Dulcolax, Senna) without doctor approval. Sitz baths provide relief if haemorrhoids develop.

How do you treat constipation in children?

Fruit (papaya, bananas, prune juice for 6+ months), plenty of water, reduce cow's milk. Gentle clockwise tummy massage. Lactulose syrup is safe but use with doctor guidance. No scolding during toilet training — patience and positive reinforcement. Use a footstool on the toilet (squatting position). If constipation lasts more than a week or is accompanied by fever or vomiting, see a doctor.

Is it safe to take Dulcolax every day?

No. Stimulant laxatives (Dulcolax, Senna) are not safe for long-term use — the colon becomes "lazy" and dependency develops. They should only be used occasionally. Long-term safe options are Isabgol (psyllium) and Lactulose. Always try to resolve constipation with fibre, water and exercise first. If you need a laxative daily, consult a gastroenterologist.
