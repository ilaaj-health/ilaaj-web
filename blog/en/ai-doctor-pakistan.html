AI Doctor in Pakistan — A New Era of Healthcare

Pakistan has one of the lowest doctor-to-patient ratios in the world — just 1 doctor per 1,000 patients. For the average citizen, accessing quality healthcare means long queues, expensive consultations, and distant clinics. Smartphones and AI have changed that equation.

How Ilaaj AI Works — 7 Simple Steps

Step 1 — Profile Setup

Create an account once. Add family members, each with their own health context.

Step 2 — Describe Your Complaint

Type your symptoms in Roman Urdu or English, or send a voice message. Example: "I've had a headache for 2 days and a mild fever" — that's all you need.

Step 3 — AI Structured Intake

The AI asks 5–7 focused questions:

  • Pain severity (severity scale)
  • Duration and location
  • Associated symptoms
  • Past medical history
  • Current medications
  • Allergies
  • Triggers or patterns

Step 4 — AI Generates a Draft Prescription

After collecting all information, the AI drafts a structured prescription including:

  • Possible diagnosis
  • Recommended medications with dosage
  • Lifestyle advice
  • Red flags / when to seek emergency care
  • Disclaimer: "This prescription was drafted by AI"

Step 5 — Payment Gate

A small payment unlocks the verified prescription — far cheaper than a clinic visit.

Step 6 — Doctor Review and Approval

The assigned PMDC-registered doctor receives:

  • Full chat history
  • AI-drafted prescription
  • Patient's medical history

The doctor approves as-is or modifies it. The prescription becomes legally valid.

Step 7 — Verified Prescription Delivered

The prescription arrives in the chat with the doctor's digital signature and PMDC number. You can collect your medicine from any pharmacy in Pakistan.

When Ilaaj AI Is Not Enough

For the following emergencies, call 1122 (Rescue Pakistan) or go to the nearest hospital ER:

  • Heart attack symptoms (chest pain, shortness of breath)
  • Stroke symptoms (facial drooping, weakness, speech difficulty)
  • Severe bleeding
  • Severe trauma or accident
  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
  • Suicide attempt or severe mental health crisis
  • Severe asthma attack
  • Diabetic emergency (DKA)

This combination delivers AI speed with a doctor's trust and legal validity. For emergencies, always go to a hospital ER.

Absolutely.
