Panadol Extra — Uses, Dosage and Side Effects Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Panadol Extra = Paracetamol 500 mg + Caffeine 65 mg
  • More effective than regular Panadol for migraines and strong headaches
  • Maximum 8 tablets per day; reduce to 6 if also consuming other caffeine
  • Consult a doctor if you have hypertension or heart problems
  • Avoid at night — caffeine disrupts sleep

What Is Panadol Extra?

Panadol Extra is GSK's combination product based on two active ingredients:

  • Paracetamol 500 mg — painkiller and antipyretic
  • Caffeine 65 mg — boosts the painkilling effect by up to 40%

What Is Panadol Extra Used For?

1. Migraine and Strong Headache

More effective than regular Panadol due to caffeine. Best taken at the very onset of a migraine attack.

2. Tension Headache

Faster relief for stress- and fatigue-related headaches.

3. Severe Body Aches

Body pain associated with flu-like conditions.

4. Period Cramps

An alternative to Brufen — the combination of caffeine and paracetamol provides effective relief.

5. Post-Surgical Pain

For mild-to-moderate pain after procedures.

6. Toothache

When extra strength is needed for dental pain.

Dosage

For Adults and Children 12 and Over:

  • Single dose: 1–2 tablets (500–1000 mg paracetamol + 65–130 mg caffeine)
  • Frequency: Every 4–6 hours
  • Maximum per 24 hours: 8 tablets (4 grams paracetamol + 520 mg caffeine)
  • Safe on empty stomach

Important Note:

If you also drink tea, coffee, or cola during the day, you need to track your total caffeine intake. The safe daily caffeine limit is 400 mg (equivalent to 2–3 cups of coffee).

Side Effects

Common (due to caffeine):

  • Restlessness or anxiety
  • Difficulty sleeping
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Frequent urination
  • Mild acidity
  • Hand tremors (at high doses)

Serious:

  • Liver damage (paracetamol overdose)
  • Severe allergic reaction
  • Heart palpitations (in caffeine-sensitive individuals)

When to Avoid Panadol Extra

  • Uncontrolled high blood pressure
  • Heart problems — arrhythmia, angina
  • Anxiety disorders
  • Insomnia or sleep problems
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding — regular Panadol is better due to the caffeine
  • Liver disease
  • Children under 12
  • Caffeine sensitivity

Drug Interactions

  • Other caffeine sources — tea, coffee, energy drinks
  • Theophylline (asthma medicine) — caffeine amplifies the effect
  • Warfarin — increased bleeding risk
  • Antibiotics (Ciprofloxacin) — caffeine is metabolised more slowly
  • Alcohol — increased liver damage risk
  • MAO inhibitors (antidepressants) — dangerous blood pressure spike

Common Use Cases in Pakistan

  • Office workers — afternoon headaches
  • Students — exam stress headaches
  • Drivers — long-drive fatigue combined with pain
  • Migraine sufferers — first response at onset
  • After breaking fast (Iftar) — caffeine withdrawal headache

Tips for Best Results

  1. Take it at the first sign of a headache — do not wait
  2. Drink plenty of water — dehydration is a major trigger for headaches in Pakistan
  3. Rest in a dark, quiet room for 30 minutes
  4. Get adequate sleep — break the caffeine-dependence cycle
  5. Identify and manage your migraine triggers

Overdose

A Panadol Extra overdose carries two separate dangers:

  1. Paracetamol toxicity: Liver damage from 10 grams or more
  2. Caffeine toxicity: Serious effects from approximately 1 gram (~15 tablets)

Symptoms include vomiting, rapid heartbeat, loss of consciousness, and seizures. Call 1122 or go to the nearest emergency room immediately.

Conclusion

Panadol Extra is the preferred OTC option for migraines and strong headaches in Pakistan — caffeine enhances the painkilling effect and provides a faster onset. However, regular Panadol is better for people sensitive to caffeine, those with hypertension, pregnant women, and anyone with sleep problems. Maximum 8 tablets per day; avoid at night. For frequent migraines, consult a doctor on Ilaaj AI for prescription-strength options such as Sumatriptan.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Panadol Extra used for?

Panadol Extra contains paracetamol 500 mg plus caffeine 65 mg. It is more effective and faster-acting than regular Panadol for strong headaches, migraines, tension headaches, body aches, period cramps, and toothache. Caffeine boosts the painkilling effect by up to 40%. For adults and children aged 12 and over, the maximum is 8 tablets per day.

How many Panadol Extra tablets can I take per day?

Adults may take 1–2 tablets every 4–6 hours, with a maximum of 8 tablets per 24 hours. This equals 4 grams of paracetamol and 520 mg of caffeine. If you also drink tea or coffee, keep your total daily caffeine under 400 mg. Do not use for more than 3 days without consulting a doctor.

Can I take Panadol Extra at night?

Avoid Panadol Extra at night due to the caffeine — it can disrupt sleep for 6–8 hours. Take your last dose at least 6 hours before bedtime. For night-time pain, regular Panadol 1000 mg or Brufen is a better option. People sensitive to caffeine should also avoid it in the evening.

Is Panadol Extra safe during pregnancy?

Regular Panadol is the better choice during pregnancy. The caffeine in Panadol Extra passes to the baby — while a small amount is safe, excessive caffeine increases the risk of miscarriage and premature birth. Keep total daily caffeine under 200 mg during pregnancy. Use plain paracetamol for pain. Brufen and Voltral must be completely avoided in the third trimester.

What is the difference between Panadol Extra and Brufen?

Panadol Extra (paracetamol + caffeine): safe on empty stomach, avoid in pregnancy, safe in dengue, for mild to moderate pain. Brufen 400 (ibuprofen): also anti-inflammatory, take after food, stronger for period cramps and joint pain, absolutely not in dengue (bleeding risk), avoid with ulcers or kidney problems. A doctor will choose based on your situation.
