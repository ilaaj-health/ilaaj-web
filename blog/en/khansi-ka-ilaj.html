Cough Treatment — Causes, Types and Best Syrup in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Dry cough and wet (chesty) cough require different treatments
  • Honey and ginger is Pakistan's time-tested home remedy
  • Never give honey to children under 1 year
  • A cough lasting more than 2 weeks requires a doctor
  • Coughing up blood or breathlessness is an emergency

What Is a Cough?

A cough is your body's defense mechanism — it clears the airway of irritants, mucus, or bacteria. In Pakistan, smog, dust, and seasonal flu are the biggest causes of coughing.

Common Causes of Cough
Viral Cold/FluLasts 5–10 days, with phlegm
Allergy / SmogDry cough, itchy eyes and nose
Acid Reflux (GERD)Night-time cough, worsens when lying down
AsthmaWheezing, coughing after exercise
TB (Tuberculosis)3+ weeks, night sweats, weight loss
Bacterial Chest InfectionYellow/green phlegm, with fever

Dry Cough Treatment

In a dry cough, no phlegm comes out — there is only a persistent tickle in the throat. The cause is usually allergy, smog, or a viral infection.

Home Remedies:

  • Honey (1 teaspoon): taken directly or in warm water. Can also be given to children over 1 year — proven by research
  • Chew a piece of ginger or have ginger tea with honey
  • Steam inhalation — 10 minutes, twice a day
  • Warm salt water gargle
  • Keep the throat moist with a humidifier or a bowl of water in the room

Medicine:

  • Cough suppressant syrup: Tixylix, Cofsils Dry Cough — dextromethorphan-based
  • Antihistamine: If caused by allergy — Telfast, Zyrtec
  • Lozenges: Strepsils or Vicks for throat soothing

Wet Cough (Chesty Cough) Treatment

A wet cough brings up mucus from the chest. This is actually productive — the body is clearing infection. The goal is to thin the mucus so it comes out more easily.

Home Remedies:

  • Plenty of warm fluids — tea, soup, warm water
  • Steam inhalation 2–3 times a day
  • Onion with honey — an old Pakistani remedy
  • Joshanda herbal drink (Hamdard, Qarshi) — helpful for colds

Medicine:

  • Expectorant syrup: Mucolite, Hydryllin, Solvin — these thin the mucus
  • Mucus thinners: Mucinex, Bisolvon
  • Antibiotics: Only on a doctor's prescription — for yellow/green phlegm or with fever

Night-Time Cough Treatment

Common causes of coughing that worsens at night:

  • Acid reflux: stop eating 3 hours before bed, keep your head elevated when sleeping
  • Postnasal drip: rinse your nose with saline before sleeping
  • Asthma: if cough worsens after exercise or in cold air, an inhaler may be needed
  • Smog: in Lahore/Karachi, use an air purifier or hang a damp cloth in the room

Cough in Children

  • Never give honey to children under 1 year — risk of botulism
  • Half a teaspoon of honey before bed is safe for children over 1 year
  • OTC cough syrups are not recommended for children under 4 years
  • Calpol can be used if fever accompanies the cough
  • Steam inhalation is safe for children — but supervise carefully to prevent burns
  • If the child is breathing very fast, turns blue, or stops breastfeeding = emergency

Persistent Cough (3+ Weeks) — What Are the Causes?

If a cough has lasted more than 3 weeks, it is called a "chronic cough." Common causes:

  • Asthma — worsens with exercise or cold weather
  • GERD / acid reflux — after meals or at night
  • Postnasal drip — mucus dripping from the nose into the throat
  • TB (Tuberculosis) — common in Pakistan, accompanied by night sweats, weight loss, blood in cough
  • Smoking
  • ACE inhibitor blood pressure medicine as a side effect (Capoten, Zestril)
  • Lung infection or, rarely, lung cancer

When to See a Doctor Immediately

  • Blood in cough
  • Shortness of breath or wheezing
  • Chest pain
  • Cough with fever above 102°F
  • Weight loss and night sweats (suspected TB)
  • Cough lasting more than 2 weeks
  • Barking cough (croup) or whooping cough in children
  • Child turning blue or stopping breathing

Cough Prevention

  • Wear an N95 mask on smoggy days
  • Wash hands regularly
  • Avoid smoking and passive smoke
  • Annual flu shot
  • Eat Vitamin C and zinc-rich foods (citrus fruits, kinno, ginger)
  • Drink plenty of water

Conclusion

The correct treatment for a cough depends on its type and cause. Dry cough and wet cough are treated differently — the wrong syrup can make things worse. Antibiotics are only necessary for bacterial infections; they are useless for viral coughs. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best cough syrup in Pakistan?

It depends on the type of cough. For dry cough, Tixylix or Cofsils Dry Cough (dextromethorphan-based) is effective. For wet cough with phlegm, Mucolite, Hydryllin, or Solvin thin the mucus. For allergy-related cough, Telfast or Zyrtec. Using the wrong syrup can make things worse — consulting a doctor is better.

How to treat a child's cough?

For children over 1 year, half a teaspoon of honey before bed is safe and effective. Use steam inhalation carefully. OTC cough syrups are not recommended for children under 4 years. If the cough is accompanied by fever, give Calpol. Never give honey to children under 1 year.

Why does coughing get worse at night?

Common causes of night-time coughing include acid reflux (acid rising to the throat when lying down), postnasal drip (mucus from the nose dripping into the throat), asthma, or smog and dust. Stop eating 3 hours before bed, keep your head slightly elevated when sleeping, and use a humidifier or damp cloth in the room. Coughing at night for more than 1 week requires a doctor.

What is the immediate remedy for a dry cough?

Take 1 teaspoon of honey directly or in warm water. Chewing a piece of ginger is also effective. Do steam inhalation for 10 minutes, twice a day. Gargle with warm salt water. Strepsils lozenges soothe the throat. If there is no improvement within 1 week, see a doctor.

What does coughing up blood mean?

Coughing up blood (hemoptysis) is a serious sign and you must see a doctor immediately. Causes include TB (common in Pakistan), bronchitis, pneumonia, or in rare cases lung cancer. This is especially concerning if accompanied by weight loss, night sweats, or coughing lasting 3+ weeks. A chest X-ray and sputum test are needed.
