Breeky Tablet Uses, Dosage and Side Effects in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Breeky = ibuprofen 400 mg + paracetamol 325 mg — dual-action pain and fever tablet
  • Used for fever, headache, period cramps, dental pain, muscle aches and back pain
  • Works faster and stronger than either drug alone due to complementary mechanisms
  • Always take with food — never on an empty stomach due to ibuprofen
  • Avoid in peptic ulcers, kidney disease, third-trimester pregnancy and dengue

What Is Breeky?

Breeky is a fixed-dose combination (FDC) tablet containing ibuprofen 400 mg and paracetamol (acetaminophen) 325 mg. This combination is prescribed and sold widely in Pakistan as a dual-mechanism pain reliever and fever reducer. The logic is straightforward: ibuprofen (an NSAID) and paracetamol work through different pathways to reduce pain and fever, so combining them at lower doses of each achieves better and faster relief than a full dose of either drug alone — while keeping the side-effect risk lower than taking maximum doses of both separately.

Breeky is manufactured by Ferozsons Laboratories (Pakistan) and is one of several ibuprofen-paracetamol combination tablets available in the country, alongside similar products from other manufacturers.

How Does Breeky Work?

The two active ingredients work through completely separate mechanisms, which is the key reason the combination is more effective than either alone:

Ibuprofen (400 mg): A non-selective COX-1 and COX-2 inhibitor. It blocks the enzyme cyclooxygenase from converting arachidonic acid into prostaglandins — the inflammatory mediators responsible for pain sensitisation, fever generation and local swelling at injury sites. This gives ibuprofen its anti-inflammatory, analgesic and antipyretic properties.

Paracetamol (325 mg): Paracetamol's precise mechanism remains an area of research, but it is believed to act centrally — inhibiting a variant of cyclooxygenase in the brain (COX-3 / central COX) and modulating the endocannabinoid and serotonin systems to raise the pain threshold. It reduces fever by acting on the hypothalamic heat-regulation centre. Crucially, paracetamol has minimal peripheral anti-inflammatory effect — it does not irritate the stomach.

Together: ibuprofen addresses peripheral inflammation and prostaglandin-driven pain; paracetamol raises the central pain threshold. Clinical trials have consistently shown that the combination provides superior analgesia to either drug at equivalent doses taken separately.

Breeky Tablet Uses

1. Fever

Breeky is very effective for reducing fever (pyrexia), particularly in adults and adolescents with moderate to high fever (38.5°C+). The ibuprofen component provides rapid antipyretic action by blocking fever-generating prostaglandins in the hypothalamus, while paracetamol directly acts on the hypothalamic temperature set-point. The combination brings fever down faster and keeps it lower for longer than paracetamol alone. However, for dengue fever specifically, Breeky (or any NSAID) should be avoided — see contraindications below.

2. Headache and Migraine

Tension headaches — the most common type in Pakistan, caused by stress, dehydration, eye strain and poor posture — respond very well to ibuprofen + paracetamol combinations. For migraine, ibuprofen has evidence as an effective abortive therapy, particularly when taken early at the onset of a migraine. The combined tablet provides faster relief than either drug alone for both tension-type and mild-to-moderate migraine headaches.

3. Menstrual Cramps (Dysmenorrhea)

Period cramps are driven by prostaglandin-induced uterine muscle contractions. Ibuprofen is among the most effective evidence-based treatments for dysmenorrhea precisely because it blocks prostaglandin synthesis at the source. The paracetamol component adds central pain relief. Breeky taken at the first sign of cramping — before the pain peaks — provides significantly better control than waiting for severe pain to develop. It is a popular choice among women in Pakistan for this indication.

4. Dental Pain

Post-extraction, post-procedure and toothache pain are driven by both local inflammation (prostaglandins) and central pain sensitisation. The ibuprofen + paracetamol combination used in Breeky closely mirrors the combination recommended by the British Dental Journal for post-dental pain management. It is highly effective and often preferred over opioid-containing combinations for dental pain in adults.

5. Muscle and Joint Pain

Muscular aches from physical exertion, minor sports injuries, neck stiffness and back pain all respond to Breeky's anti-inflammatory + analgesic combination. Ibuprofen addresses the local inflammation at the muscle or joint, while paracetamol provides central pain relief. For acute muscle pain (e.g., after gym, heavy lifting), Breeky is more effective than paracetamol alone.

6. Ear Pain (Otitis Externa / Otitis Media)

Ear infections cause significant pain through inflammation. While Breeky does not treat the underlying infection, it provides meaningful symptomatic relief from the pain and associated fever while antibiotics take effect.

Important: Do not take other paracetamol-containing products (Panadol, Panadol CF, Panadol Extra, cough syrups, flu tablets) while taking Breeky — this can lead to paracetamol overdose, which causes serious liver damage.

Breeky Side Effects

Common (from ibuprofen component):

  • Gastric pain, nausea, indigestion — most common; always take with food
  • Acid reflux / heartburn
  • Headache or dizziness — mild

Less common / important:

  • Gastric ulcer or bleeding — risk increases with regular use, empty stomach, or in elderly patients
  • Kidney impairment — NSAIDs reduce renal blood flow; risk higher in dehydration and existing kidney disease
  • Elevated blood pressure — ibuprofen can raise BP, especially with long-term use
  • Fluid retention (oedema)
  • Liver injury from paracetamol overdose — if paracetamol is double-dosed by taking other products simultaneously
  • Allergic reaction — rare; stop immediately if rash, breathing difficulty or facial swelling

Who Should NOT Take Breeky

  • Active peptic ulcer or GI bleeding history — ibuprofen can worsen both
  • Dengue fever — NSAIDs increase bleeding risk in dengue; use paracetamol alone
  • Kidney disease (CKD) — ibuprofen reduces renal blood flow; avoid or use with extreme caution
  • Liver disease — paracetamol is hepatotoxic at higher doses; reduce dose or avoid
  • Third trimester of pregnancy (weeks 28–40) — ibuprofen causes premature ductus arteriosus closure; strictly contraindicated
  • Severe heart failure — NSAIDs worsen fluid retention
  • Asthma triggered by NSAIDs — ~10% of asthmatics are aspirin/NSAID sensitive
  • Allergy to ibuprofen, aspirin or paracetamol
  • Children under 16

Drug Interactions

  • Warfarin / blood thinners: ibuprofen increases bleeding risk — avoid combination
  • Antihypertensives (ACE inhibitors, ARBs, diuretics): ibuprofen reduces their effectiveness and increases kidney risk
  • Other NSAIDs (aspirin, diclofenac): additive gastric toxicity — do not combine
  • Other paracetamol products: risk of paracetamol overdose — check every other tablet you take
  • Lithium: ibuprofen raises lithium levels — avoid
  • Methotrexate: ibuprofen raises methotrexate toxicity — avoid

Conclusion

Breeky (ibuprofen 400 mg + paracetamol 325 mg) is one of the most effective OTC pain and fever tablets in Pakistan, combining two drugs with different mechanisms to provide superior analgesic and antipyretic effect. It is particularly well-suited for moderate pain from menstrual cramps, dental procedures, headaches and muscle aches. The golden rule is always take with food and never exceed 3 tablets per day. Do not take alongside other paracetamol-containing products, and absolutely avoid in dengue fever, active peptic ulcer, kidney disease and third-trimester pregnancy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Breeky tablet used for?

Breeky (ibuprofen 400mg + paracetamol 325mg) is used for fever, headache, menstrual cramps, dental pain, muscle aches and back pain. The combination works faster and stronger than either drug alone by targeting pain through two different mechanisms.

Can I take Breeky on an empty stomach?

No — Breeky must always be taken with food or milk. The ibuprofen component can irritate the stomach lining and increase risk of gastric ulcers when taken on an empty stomach.

How often can I take Breeky?

One tablet every 6–8 hours as needed, maximum 3 tablets per day. Do not use for more than 3 consecutive days without medical advice.

Can I take Breeky in dengue fever?

No — absolutely not. NSAIDs (including ibuprofen in Breeky) increase bleeding risk in dengue fever, which can be life-threatening. Use paracetamol alone for fever in dengue. Do not take Breeky, Brufen, Synflex or any NSAID if dengue is suspected.

Can I take Panadol with Breeky?

No — Breeky already contains 325 mg paracetamol. Taking Panadol alongside Breeky risks paracetamol overdose, which causes serious liver damage. Check all other tablets and syrups you are taking for paracetamol content before combining.
