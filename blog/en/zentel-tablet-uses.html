Zentel Tablet Uses, Dosage and Side Effects in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Zentel = albendazole 400mg, a broad-spectrum deworming tablet (anthelmintic)
  • Treats roundworm, hookworm, whipworm, pinworm, and more — often in a single dose
  • Absolutely contraindicated in pregnancy — teratogenic
  • For pinworm: treat the whole family and wash all bedding simultaneously
  • Best absorbed when taken with a fatty meal; repeat dose after 2 weeks for best results

What Is Zentel?

Zentel is the brand name for albendazole 400mg, a broad-spectrum anthelmintic (deworming medicine) belonging to the benzimidazole class of drugs. It works by disrupting the formation of microtubules inside worm cells — the structural proteins the worm needs to absorb glucose and survive. Without the ability to take up nutrients, the worm is paralysed and dies within days. Zentel is manufactured by GSK (GlaxoSmithKline) and is widely available at pharmacies across Pakistan. It comes as a standard tablet and as a chewable tablet, making it easy to give to children.

Intestinal worm infections are extremely common in Pakistan — particularly among children, in rural and peri-urban areas, and in communities where hand hygiene, clean water, and proper sanitation are limited. The WHO estimates that hundreds of millions of children in low- and middle-income countries are affected by soil-transmitted helminths at any given time, and Pakistan remains one of the higher-burden countries. A single Zentel tablet, costing less than 50 PKR, can eliminate the most common worm infections rapidly and safely.

How Does Albendazole (Zentel) Work?

Albendazole belongs to the benzimidazole anthelmintic class, which also includes mebendazole (the active ingredient in Vermox). The mechanism of action is well understood: albendazole binds selectively to beta-tubulin in helminth (worm) cells and inhibits its polymerisation into microtubules. Microtubules are essential for glucose uptake, cellular structure, and reproduction in worms. Disrupting them causes the worm to be unable to absorb nutrients, leading to progressive paralysis and death. Because the drug has much higher affinity for worm beta-tubulin than for human beta-tubulin, it is selectively toxic to the parasites at normal therapeutic doses.

Albendazole is also converted in the liver to its active metabolite, albendazole sulfoxide, which reaches systemic circulation and can act on tissue-dwelling worms — making it more effective than mebendazole for infections that have spread beyond the gut (such as neurocysticercosis or hydatid disease).

Zentel Tablet Uses

1. Intestinal Roundworm (Ascariasis)

Ascaris lumbricoides is the most common intestinal worm worldwide. Children in Pakistan frequently become infected by ingesting eggs from contaminated soil, vegetables, or water. Heavy infections cause abdominal pain, nutritional deficiency, and stunted growth. A single dose of Zentel 400mg is highly effective — cure rates exceed 90% in most studies.

2. Hookworm Infection (Ancylostoma, Necator)

Hookworms penetrate through the skin (usually bare feet on contaminated soil) and migrate to the small intestine, where they attach and feed on blood. Chronic hookworm infection is a significant cause of iron-deficiency anaemia in Pakistan, particularly in rural children and agricultural workers. Zentel 400mg single dose achieves cure rates of 70–90%. A repeat dose after 2 weeks improves efficacy.

3. Whipworm (Trichuriasis)

Trichuris trichiura infects the large intestine and causes bloody diarrhoea, rectal prolapse in severe cases, and chronic nutritional impairment in children. Albendazole is effective though cure rates for whipworm with a single dose are somewhat lower than for roundworm — 3-day courses (400mg daily for 3 days) may be preferred for heavy infections.

4. Pinworm / Threadworm (Enterobiasis)

Enterobius vermicularis is the most common worm infection in Pakistan's urban population, especially among school-age children. The hallmark symptom is intense anal itching at night (when the female worm migrates to lay eggs around the anus). Children may also have disturbed sleep, irritability, and teeth grinding. A single dose of Zentel 400mg is highly effective, but because pinworm eggs survive in the environment for weeks, a repeat dose after 2 weeks is essential — as is treating all household members simultaneously and washing all bedding and clothing.

5. Strongyloidiasis

Strongyloides stercoralis is a soil-transmitted worm that can auto-infect and persist for decades. It is particularly dangerous in immunocompromised patients (those on steroids, chemotherapy, or with HIV). Albendazole is effective, though ivermectin is often preferred for strongyloidiasis when available.

6. Giardiasis (Off-Label)

Giardia is a protozoan (not a worm), but albendazole has demonstrated efficacy against Giardia lamblia in clinical studies and is used off-label in Pakistan, often because it is more readily available than metronidazole or tinidazole in some settings. Standard treatment is 400mg once daily for 5 days.

7. Neurocysticercosis (Specialist Use)

Neurocysticercosis is caused by Taenia solium (pork tapeworm) larvae lodging in the brain. It is a significant cause of seizures in Pakistan. Treatment requires albendazole 400mg twice daily for 8–30 days, always in combination with corticosteroids to manage the inflammatory response to dying larvae, and under specialist neurological supervision. This is not a condition to self-treat.

8. Hydatid Disease / Echinococcosis (Specialist Use)

Hydatid cysts caused by Echinococcus granulosus (acquired from dogs) are treated with prolonged albendazole courses (400mg twice daily in 28-day cycles with 14-day breaks, repeated for months) alongside surgical or radiological intervention. This requires specialist hepatology or surgery input.

Pakistan Context — Why Deworming Matters

Pakistan faces a high burden of intestinal worm infections due to a combination of factors: widespread open defecation in rural areas, contaminated water supplies, inadequate sanitation infrastructure, frequent consumption of raw vegetables irrigated with contaminated water, and limited access to handwashing facilities in schools. Studies in Pakistan's Punjab and KPK provinces have found prevalence rates of soil-transmitted helminth infections ranging from 20% to over 60% in school-age children.

The consequences go beyond discomfort. Heavy worm burdens in children cause iron-deficiency anaemia, protein malnutrition, impaired cognitive development, and reduced school attendance. The WHO recommends periodic preventive deworming (prophylactic albendazole every 6 months) for all children aged 1–14 in areas where helminth prevalence exceeds 20% — which includes large parts of Pakistan. Zentel is the drug used in Pakistan's national school deworming programmes.

Important dosing note: Zentel's absorption from the gut is markedly increased when taken with a fatty meal. Studies show absorption can be 4–5 times higher after a high-fat meal compared to fasting. For simple intestinal worms, this may not be critical at standard doses, but for tissue worms (neurocysticercosis, hydatid) where systemic levels matter, taking Zentel with a fatty meal is essential.

Side Effects

Single-Dose (Intestinal Worms) — Usually Minimal

For a single 400mg dose used to treat common intestinal worms, side effects are generally mild and transient:

  • Mild nausea or stomach discomfort
  • Headache
  • Mild abdominal pain or diarrhoea (may partly be from dying worms)
  • Dizziness

Most people experience no significant side effects from a single dose.

Long Courses (Neurocysticercosis, Hydatid) — Monitor Carefully

Prolonged courses carry greater risk of more serious effects:

  • Elevated liver enzymes (hepatotoxicity) — liver function tests must be monitored monthly
  • Bone marrow suppression (leucopenia, thrombocytopenia) — rare but serious; full blood count should be checked
  • Alopecia (hair loss) — reversible on stopping treatment
  • Severe allergic reactions — rare
  • CNS effects in neurocysticercosis — headache, seizures (from inflammatory reaction to dying larvae — managed with corticosteroids)

Contraindications — Who Should NOT Take Zentel

  • Pregnancy — absolute contraindication. Albendazole is teratogenic. Do not use in pregnancy or in women who may be pregnant. Confirm negative pregnancy before treatment in women of childbearing age. Use reliable contraception during and for at least 1 month after completing treatment.
  • Liver disease — use with caution; dose adjustment or avoidance in significant hepatic impairment. Liver function should be checked before and during prolonged courses.
  • Hypersensitivity to albendazole or other benzimidazoles
  • Children under 1 year — not recommended

Pinworm — Why You Must Treat the Whole Family

Pinworm (threadworm, Enterobius vermicularis) is highly contagious within households. The female worm lays eggs around the anus at night — these eggs are transferred to fingernails when the child scratches, spread to surfaces, bedding, clothing, and can survive for up to 2–3 weeks in the environment. Re-infection is almost guaranteed unless all household members are treated at the same time, even if they have no symptoms. In addition to a single Zentel dose (repeated at 2 weeks for everyone):

  • Wash all bedding and clothing in hot water on the day of treatment
  • Keep fingernails short and clean
  • Reinforce handwashing after toilet use and before meals
  • Avoid shaking bedding to prevent egg dispersal in the air

Prevention — Reducing Worm Infections in Pakistan

Deworming treats the active infection, but re-infection is likely without attention to hygiene and environmental factors:

  • Handwashing: Wash hands thoroughly with soap before eating and after using the toilet. This single measure dramatically reduces transmission of soil-transmitted helminths and pinworm.
  • Clean water: Use filtered or boiled water for drinking and food preparation.
  • Cook food thoroughly: Raw or undercooked vegetables grown in contaminated soil are a common source of worm eggs. Wash all vegetables before eating.
  • Footwear: Wearing shoes prevents hookworm larvae from penetrating through the skin.
  • Periodic deworming: WHO recommends albendazole 400mg every 6 months for all children aged 1–14 in endemic areas. Many schools in Pakistan participate in government deworming campaigns.
  • Proper sanitation: Using designated toilets and not defecating in open fields prevents environmental contamination with worm eggs.

Zentel vs Mebendazole (Vermox) — Which Is Better?

Both Zentel (albendazole) and Vermox (mebendazole) are benzimidazole anthelmintics with a similar mechanism of action. The key differences:

  • Convenience: Zentel works as a single 400mg dose for most intestinal worms; mebendazole (Vermox) traditionally requires a 3-day course (100mg twice daily) for roundworm, hookworm, and whipworm — though single-dose mebendazole 500mg is also available.
  • Systemic absorption: Albendazole is much better absorbed systemically (converted to albendazole sulfoxide in the liver). This makes Zentel the preferred drug for tissue-dwelling worms like those causing neurocysticercosis and hydatid disease, where systemic levels matter. Mebendazole is poorly absorbed and acts mainly in the gut lumen.
  • Whipworm efficacy: Both drugs have lower single-dose cure rates for whipworm compared to roundworm — a 3-day albendazole course is often more effective for heavy whipworm infections.
  • Availability and cost: Both are widely available in Pakistan. Zentel (GSK brand) is the most recognised. Generic albendazole 400mg tablets are also available at lower cost.

For most practical purposes in Pakistan — treating a child or family member for common intestinal worms — a single Zentel 400mg tablet is the most convenient and effective choice.

When to See a Doctor

For most common intestinal worm infections in otherwise healthy adults and children over 2 years, Zentel can be taken without a prescription. However, consult a doctor if:

  • You are unsure which worm is causing the infection — different symptoms may suggest different parasites requiring different treatment
  • The patient is a child under 2 years — dose adjustment required
  • There is a possibility of pregnancy
  • Symptoms are severe — significant weight loss, severe anaemia, high fever, neurological symptoms (seizures, severe headaches) — which may indicate a more serious infection
  • Symptoms do not resolve after two courses of Zentel
  • The patient is immunocompromised (HIV, on steroids or chemotherapy) — worm infections can behave differently and may require specialist evaluation

Conclusion

Zentel (albendazole 400mg) is one of Pakistan's most important and affordable medicines for controlling intestinal worm infections. A single tablet eliminates the most common worms affecting children and adults — roundworm, hookworm, whipworm, and pinworm — safely and effectively. For pinworm, always treat the whole family and follow strict hygiene measures to prevent re-infection. The absolute contraindication in pregnancy must be respected. For more serious tissue worm infections like neurocysticercosis or hydatid disease, Zentel is effective but must always be supervised by a specialist. Following up deworming with good handwashing habits and periodic preventive deworming every 6 months — as recommended by the WHO — is the key to long-term protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Zentel tablet used for?

Zentel (albendazole 400mg) is a broad-spectrum deworming tablet used to treat intestinal worm infections including roundworm (ascariasis), hookworm, whipworm (trichuriasis), pinworm (enterobiasis), and strongyloidiasis. It is also used for more serious tissue infections such as neurocysticercosis and hydatid disease (echinococcosis), though these require longer specialist-supervised courses.

What is the correct Zentel dose for adults and children in Pakistan?

For most common intestinal worms in adults and children over 2 years: a single dose of 400mg, repeated after 2 weeks. For pinworm, the whole family must be treated simultaneously. Children under 2 years receive 200mg. Zentel is best taken with a fatty meal to improve absorption. For neurocysticercosis or hydatid disease, specialist supervision is mandatory.

Can Zentel be taken during pregnancy?

No — Zentel (albendazole) is absolutely contraindicated in pregnancy. It is teratogenic (causes birth defects in animal studies) and must not be used during pregnancy or by women who may become pregnant during treatment. A pregnancy test is recommended before starting Zentel in women of childbearing age, and effective contraception must be used during and for at least one month after treatment.

How long does it take for Zentel to work?

For common intestinal worms, Zentel kills the worms within 1–3 days of a single dose, though dead worms and eggs may continue to pass in stool for several days. Symptoms such as abdominal discomfort and disturbed sleep from pinworm typically improve within a few days. A second dose after 2 weeks is recommended to kill any newly hatched worms.

Is Zentel safe for children?

Yes — Zentel is safe and commonly used in children over 2 years at the full adult dose of 400mg for intestinal worms. Children aged 1–2 years may be given 200mg under medical advice. Children under 1 year should not receive Zentel. The WHO includes albendazole in its essential medicines list and recommends periodic deworming for all school-age children in endemic areas including Pakistan.
