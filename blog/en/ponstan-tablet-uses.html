Ponstan 250/500mg (Mefenamic Acid) — Uses, Dosage and Side Effects in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Ponstan = mefenamic acid, NSAID class
  • Highly effective for period cramps — also reduces heavy bleeding
  • 250mg three times daily, maximum 7 days
  • Always take with food to protect your stomach
  • Avoid in 3rd trimester of pregnancy and with kidney disease

What is Ponstan?

Ponstan is Pfizer's brand name for mefenamic acid. It belongs to the NSAID (Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drug) class and is specifically very effective for period cramps. It is the most commonly used period painkiller among women in Pakistan.

What is Ponstan Used For?

1. Period Cramps (Most Common)

The gold-standard for dysmenorrhea. Start 1–2 days before your period begins for best results.

2. Heavy Periods (Menorrhagia)

Reduces menstrual bleeding by 30–50% by lowering inflammation in the uterine lining.

3. Headache / Migraine

Effective for tension headaches and migraines.

4. Toothache

Especially useful for post-extraction dental pain.

5. Joint and Muscle Pain

Mild arthritis, sports injuries and soft tissue sprains.

6. Fever

Has an antipyretic effect, though Panadol or Brufen are preferred first choices for fever.

7. Inflammation

Soft tissue injuries and sprains.

Period Cramps Tip:

Start Ponstan 1–2 days before your period begins and continue for the first 2–3 days. Don't wait for the pain to become severe — preventive use is far more effective.

Side Effects

Common:

  • Stomach pain, nausea
  • Acidity, heartburn
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Headache
  • Drowsiness
  • Skin rash

Serious (Rare):

  • Stomach ulcer and bleeding
  • Kidney damage
  • Liver problems
  • Severe allergic reaction
  • Asthma exacerbation
  • Stevens-Johnson syndrome

When to Avoid Ponstan

  • 3rd trimester of pregnancy — fetal heart complications
  • Active stomach ulcer
  • Severe kidney disease
  • Heart failure
  • Aspirin or NSAID allergy
  • Severe asthma
  • Bleeding disorders
  • Children under 14 years

Drug Interactions

  • Warfarin: Increases bleeding risk
  • BP medicines (ACE inhibitors): Reduced effectiveness
  • Lithium: Risk of toxicity
  • Methotrexate: Risk of toxicity
  • Other NSAIDs/Aspirin: Avoid combination
  • Steroids: Increased stomach ulcer risk

Pregnancy and Breastfeeding

  • 1st trimester: Avoid
  • 2nd trimester: Short-term use only under medical supervision
  • 3rd trimester: ABSOLUTELY avoid — risk of fetal heart and kidney complications
  • Breastfeeding: Generally safe for short-term use

Tips for Period Pain Management

  1. Start Ponstan 1–2 days before your period begins
  2. Always take with food
  3. Apply a warm compress to your abdomen for 15–20 minutes
  4. Take a warm bath
  5. Light exercise (walking, yoga)
  6. Reduce caffeine intake
  7. Magnesium and omega-3 supplements (long-term benefit)
  8. If pain is severe, see a gynecologist (check for endometriosis)

Warning Signs of Endometriosis

If Ponstan or Brufen are not providing relief, watch for:

  • Periods lasting 7+ days
  • Needing to change a pad every 1–2 hours
  • Pain during sex
  • Difficulty conceiving
  • Bowel or bladder symptoms during periods

These may indicate endometriosis — an ultrasound and evaluation by a gynecologist is necessary.

Start it before your period begins, take it with food, and don't use it for more than 7 days without a doctor. Frequently Asked Questions

What is Ponstan tablet used for?

Ponstan (mefenamic acid) is an NSAID most prescribed in Pakistan for period cramps. It also reduces heavy menstrual bleeding by 30–50%. Other uses include headache, toothache, joint pain, muscle pain, mild arthritis and post-surgical pain. For adults and those 14 years and older. Avoid in the third trimester of pregnancy.

When should Ponstan be taken for period cramps?

For best results, start Ponstan 1–2 days before your period begins and continue for the first 2–3 days. Starting dose is 500mg, then 250mg every 6 hours. Take with food. Don't wait for pain to worsen — preventive use is far more effective. Combine with a warm compress, good hydration and adequate rest.

How many Ponstan tablets per day?

For adults: 250–500mg three times daily. Maximum 1,500mg per day (3 tablets of 500mg or 6 tablets of 250mg). For period cramps: 500mg as the starting dose, then 250mg every 6 hours. Always take with food. Maximum 7 days without a doctor's consultation.

What are the side effects of Ponstan?

Common: stomach pain, nausea, acidity, heartburn, diarrhea or constipation, headache, drowsiness. Serious (rare): stomach ulcer, kidney damage, liver problems, severe allergic reaction, asthma exacerbation. To reduce risk: take with food, drink plenty of water, don't use for more than 7 days, avoid with kidney or liver problems.

Is Ponstan safe in pregnancy?

Absolutely NOT in the 3rd trimester — risk of fetal heart complications (ductus arteriosus closure), kidney damage and delayed labor. Avoid in the 1st trimester too. Short-term use in the 2nd trimester may be acceptable only under strict medical supervision. Panadol (paracetamol) is the safest painkiller during pregnancy. Ponstan is generally safe for short-term use while breastfeeding.
