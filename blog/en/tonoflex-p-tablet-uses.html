Tonoflex P Tablet Uses, Dosage and Side Effects in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Tonoflex P = a neurotropic B-vitamin complex: thiamine (B1) + pyridoxine (B6) + cyanocobalamin (B12)
  • Used for peripheral neuropathy (diabetic, alcoholic), B vitamin deficiency, burning feet syndrome, neuralgia and carpal tunnel support
  • Usual dose: one tablet 1–3 times daily; 1–2 tablets/day for maintenance, with or after food
  • Very safe — water-soluble vitamins, excess is excreted; not a controlled drug and not habit-forming
  • Nerve symptoms improve slowly over weeks; severe B12 deficiency may still need injections first

What Is Tonoflex P?

Tonoflex P is a combination of three neurotropic B vitamins — thiamine (vitamin B1), pyridoxine (vitamin B6) and cyanocobalamin (vitamin B12). "Neurotropic" simply means these vitamins have a particular affinity for the nervous system: they are essential for the normal structure, metabolism and repair of nerve cells. In Pakistan, Tonoflex P and similar B-complex products are among the most commonly prescribed supplements for nerve-related complaints such as tingling, numbness, burning feet and general nerve weakness.

Unlike a painkiller, Tonoflex P does not block pain signals directly. Instead, it supplies the raw materials your nerves need to function and regenerate. This is why it is considered a supportive, restorative therapy rather than a fast-acting analgesic — it works gradually by correcting or preventing B-vitamin shortfalls that contribute to nerve damage.

How Does Tonoflex P Work?

Each of the three B vitamins in Tonoflex P plays a distinct but complementary role in keeping nerves healthy:

Thiamine (vitamin B1) is critical for energy production in nerve cells and for the proper conduction of nerve impulses. Severe B1 deficiency causes beriberi, a classic disease in which peripheral neuropathy, muscle weakness and, in advanced cases, heart failure develop. Even mild B1 shortfalls — common with poor diet, heavy alcohol use or chronic illness — can impair nerve function.

Pyridoxine (vitamin B6) is needed to synthesise neurotransmitters (the chemical messengers nerves use to communicate) and to form the protective myelin sheath around nerve fibres. A deficiency of B6 leads to peripheral neuropathy and skin and mucous-membrane changes.

Cyanocobalamin (vitamin B12) is essential for myelin formation and for healthy nerve and blood-cell production. B12 deficiency is a well-recognised cause of peripheral neuropathy and a particular type of nerve damage affecting the spinal cord. By supplying B12, Tonoflex P helps protect and repair myelin and supports nerve regeneration.

Together, these vitamins address several of the nutritional pathways involved in nerve damage at once, which is the rationale behind combining them in a single tablet rather than giving them separately.

Tonoflex P Tablet Uses

1. Peripheral Neuropathy (Diabetic and Alcoholic Nerve Damage)

This is the most common reason Tonoflex P is prescribed in Pakistan. Diabetic peripheral neuropathy — tingling, burning, numbness and pain in the feet and hands — is extremely common because diabetes is widespread and is often diagnosed late. B vitamins support nerve metabolism and may slow the progression of symptoms, particularly when a deficiency is contributing. Alcoholic neuropathy is another important use: chronic alcohol intake both damages nerves directly and severely depletes thiamine and other B vitamins, so replacement is a core part of treatment.

2. B Vitamin Deficiency

Poor dietary intake, malabsorption, chronic illness, pregnancy and certain medications can all cause B-vitamin deficiency. Symptoms range from fatigue, mouth ulcers and a sore tongue to numbness, tingling and weakness. Tonoflex P is a convenient way to replace B1, B6 and B12 together. In Pakistan, where vegetarian or low-meat diets and limited access to varied nutrition are common in some groups, B12 deficiency in particular is frequently encountered.

3. Carpal Tunnel Syndrome (Adjunct Support)

Carpal tunnel syndrome — compression of the median nerve at the wrist, causing tingling and numbness in the hand — is sometimes managed with B-vitamin supplementation as an adjunct alongside wrist splinting, activity modification and, where needed, surgery. Vitamin B6 has historically been used for this purpose. Tonoflex P is not a cure for carpal tunnel, but it can be part of supportive nerve care.

4. Burning Feet Syndrome and Neuralgia

"Burning feet syndrome" — a sensation of heat, tingling and aching in the soles, often worse at night — is frequently linked to B-vitamin deficiency and was historically associated with B-complex shortfalls. Tonoflex P is widely used to address the nutritional component. Likewise, in various forms of neuralgia (nerve pain), B vitamins are used as supportive therapy alongside the specific treatment for the underlying cause.

5. Support During Metformin and Antibiotic Therapy

Long-term metformin (the most commonly used diabetes medicine in Pakistan) is known to reduce B12 absorption over time, which can worsen or trigger neuropathy. Repeated or prolonged antibiotic courses can also deplete B vitamins by altering gut bacteria that contribute to B-vitamin balance. Doctors often add Tonoflex P or a similar B-complex to protect nerve health in these situations and after nerve injury.

Doses can vary between brands and formulations, so always follow the specific instructions on your pack and from your prescriber. Because the vitamins are water-soluble, there is a wide safety margin, but more is not automatically better — the goal is to correct a shortfall, not to mega-dose.

Tonoflex P Side Effects

Tonoflex P is one of the better-tolerated products you will encounter, because B vitamins are water-soluble and excess is excreted. Side effects are uncommon and usually mild:

Common:

  • Mild stomach upset or nausea — usually avoided by taking the tablet with or after food
  • Harmless change in urine colour — riboflavin and other B vitamins can give urine a brighter yellow tint; this is normal and not a cause for concern

Less Common / Rare:

  • Allergic reaction — rare; rash, itching or, very rarely, more serious hypersensitivity. Stop and seek care if this occurs
  • Pyridoxine (B6) toxicity with very high long-term doses — taking large amounts of vitamin B6 (generally above 200 mg/day) for extended periods can paradoxically cause sensory neuropathy. This is unlikely at standard Tonoflex P doses but is the reason you should not combine multiple high-dose B6 products without advice

There is no dependence, no sedation and no withdrawal with Tonoflex P. It does not impair driving and does not interact dangerously with most everyday medicines.

Who Should Be Cautious with Tonoflex P

  • People already taking other high-dose B6 supplements — avoid stacking multiple high-dose pyridoxine products to prevent cumulative B6 toxicity
  • Patients on levodopa for Parkinson's disease (older formulations) — high-dose B6 can reduce the effect of plain levodopa; discuss with your doctor (combined levodopa-carbidopa products are less affected)
  • Severe B12 deficiency or pernicious anaemia — oral tablets may not be enough; injections are often needed first to correct levels quickly
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding — B vitamins are generally considered safe and are often recommended, but confirm the specific product and dose with your doctor
  • Known allergy — anyone who has reacted to a B-vitamin product before should avoid it

Drug Interactions

Tonoflex P has very few clinically significant interactions, which is one of its advantages:

  • Levodopa (plain, without carbidopa) — vitamin B6 can reduce its effectiveness; use combined levodopa-carbidopa or seek advice
  • Certain antibiotics and anti-tuberculosis drugs (e.g. isoniazid) — isoniazid can deplete B6; B6 supplementation is often given deliberately, but the dose should be coordinated by your doctor
  • Metformin — no harmful interaction; in fact B12 supplementation is often used because metformin lowers B12 over time
  • No significant interactions with common medicines such as paracetamol, antihistamines, PPIs or most antibiotics

Methycobal contains only methylcobalamin — the ready-to-use, active form of B12 — and is favoured when the problem is specifically B12-related nerve damage. Tonoflex P and Neurobion are conceptually similar B-complex products that give broader support across three B vitamins. None of these is universally "best"; the right choice depends on whether you need targeted B12 repair or general B-vitamin support, which your doctor can determine.

Availability and Cost in Pakistan

Tonoflex P and equivalent B-complex products are widely stocked in pharmacies across Pakistan, including major chains and local medical stores. As a nutritional supplement it is usually easy to obtain, with prices commonly in the range of roughly 120–300 PKR per strip depending on pack size and brand. Even though it is generally well tolerated and often available without a prescription, it is best taken under a doctor's guidance so the underlying cause of your nerve symptoms — for example uncontrolled diabetes or true B12 deficiency — is properly identified and treated rather than masked.

Conclusion

Tonoflex P is a safe, well-tolerated neurotropic B-vitamin complex (thiamine B1 + pyridoxine B6 + cyanocobalamin B12) widely used in Pakistan for peripheral neuropathy, B-vitamin deficiency, burning feet syndrome, neuralgia and supportive nerve care — including for patients on long-term metformin or repeated antibiotics. Because the vitamins are water-soluble, it has a wide safety margin and is not habit-forming, but it works gradually over weeks rather than instantly. It is supportive rather than curative: severe B12 deficiency may still need injections first, and uncontrolled diabetes or alcohol use must be addressed for nerve symptoms to truly improve. If you have persistent tingling, numbness or nerve pain, see a doctor so the root cause is identified and the right form of B-vitamin therapy is chosen for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Tonoflex P used for?

Tonoflex P is a neurotropic B-vitamin complex (thiamine B1 + pyridoxine B6 + cyanocobalamin B12). It is used in Pakistan for peripheral neuropathy (including diabetic and alcoholic nerve damage), B vitamin deficiency, burning feet syndrome, neuralgia and as adjunct support for carpal tunnel syndrome. It also supports nerve health in people on long-term metformin or repeated antibiotic courses, which can deplete B vitamins.

Is Tonoflex P safe to take long term?

Yes. Tonoflex P contains water-soluble B vitamins, and any excess the body does not need is excreted in the urine rather than stored, so it has a wide safety margin and is frequently prescribed for months. The one caution is vitamin B6: very high doses above 200 mg/day for long periods can paradoxically cause nerve damage, but this is extremely unlikely at standard Tonoflex P doses.

What is the difference between Tonoflex P and Methycobal?

Methycobal contains only methylcobalamin — the active form of B12 — and is more targeted for B12-specific nerve damage. Tonoflex P provides broader support with three B vitamins (B1, B6 and the cyanocobalamin form of B12). For pure B12 deficiency neuropathy, methylcobalamin is often preferred; for general support, deficiency and mixed neuropathy, the combined Tonoflex P formula is useful. Your doctor will choose based on your cause.

Can Tonoflex P replace B12 injections in severe deficiency?

Not always. In severe B12 deficiency — especially pernicious anaemia or impaired gut absorption — injectable B12 is needed first to rapidly restore levels, because oral absorption may be inadequate. Oral Tonoflex P is excellent for mild deficiency, maintenance after injections and general nerve support, but it cannot substitute for injections in true malabsorption. Your doctor should check your B12 level to decide.
