Depression Treatment — Causes, Symptoms and Treatment in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Depression is a real medical illness — not a character weakness or "just a mood"
  • Over 30 million people in Pakistan live with depression or anxiety
  • Talking therapy (CBT) combined with medication is the most effective treatment
  • SSRI medicines (Prozac, Sertraline, Cipralex) are safe and effective
  • If you have suicidal thoughts, call 1166 (Umang) or 1093 immediately

Depression — An Illness, Not a Weakness

Depression is not simply "sadness" or "weakness" — it is a genuine medical condition involving brain chemistry. More than 30 million people in Pakistan live with depression or anxiety. Due to stigma, many delay seeking treatment — which allows the condition to worsen over time.

Symptoms of Depression

If the following symptoms persist for more than two weeks, clinical depression should be suspected:

Emotional Symptoms:

  • Prolonged sadness or emptiness
  • Loss of interest in activities that previously brought pleasure
  • Overwhelming hopelessness or guilt
  • Feeling worthless
  • Irritability and anger
  • Crying or feeling on the verge of tears

Physical Symptoms:

  • Sleeping too much or too little
  • Eating too much or too little (changes in weight)
  • Constant fatigue
  • Headaches, stomach pain, or body pain without apparent cause
  • Slowed movement or restlessness
  • Difficulty concentrating

Warning Signs (Seek Help Immediately):

  • Thoughts of suicide
  • Feeling that others would be better off without you
  • Self-harm
  • Making plans to end your life

Causes of Depression

  • Brain chemistry: Imbalance of serotonin and dopamine
  • Genetics: Family history
  • Stress: Job loss, divorce, financial hardship
  • Trauma: Childhood or adult abuse, accidents
  • Physical illness: Diabetes, thyroid disorders, cancer, chronic pain
  • Hormones: Postpartum depression, menopause, PMS
  • Loneliness: Social isolation
  • Substance abuse: Drugs and alcohol
  • Medications: Steroids, BP medicines, birth control pills

Depression Treatment — A Multi-Level Approach

1. Talking Therapy (Most Important)

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is the most evidence-based approach. It helps patients identify and change negative thought patterns. Clinical psychologists are available in Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad. Online therapy is equally effective.

Typical course: once a week, 12–20 sessions.

2. Medication (SSRIs Are the Most Common)

These medicines are effective and safe when prescribed by a doctor:

  • Fluoxetine (Prozac): Most commonly used, proven safe
  • Sertraline (Zoloft): Also effective for anxiety
  • Escitalopram (Lexapro, Cipralex): Newer with fewer side effects
  • Mirtazapine (Remeron): Improves sleep and appetite
  • Venlafaxine (Effexor): SNRI, used for severe cases

Important points:

  • Results take 4–6 weeks to appear
  • Some side effects (nausea, headache) may occur in the first two weeks — they usually resolve
  • Do not stop abruptly — withdrawal symptoms can occur; taper under doctor guidance
  • Typical course: 6–12 months
  • These medicines are not addictive — a common misconception

3. Lifestyle (The Foundation)

  • Exercise: 30 minutes, 3 days per week — as effective as antidepressants in mild cases
  • Sleep: 7–9 hours on a regular schedule
  • Diet: Mediterranean diet (fish, vegetables, healthy fats) supports brain health
  • Sunlight: 15–20 minutes of morning sun daily (vitamin D)
  • Social connection: Friends and family — break the cycle of isolation
  • Avoid substances: Alcohol and drugs worsen depression

Anxiety — Often Occurs Alongside Depression

Common anxiety symptoms:

  • Constant, excessive worry
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Fast or shallow breathing
  • Sweating
  • "Butterflies" in the stomach
  • Poor concentration
  • Fear that something bad will happen, for no clear reason

Treatment is similar to depression — CBT plus SSRIs are effective. Lexotanil (bromazepam) may be used short-term for acute panic attacks, but it can be habit-forming.

Mental Health Resources in Pakistan

  • Umang (1166): 24/7 crisis helpline
  • Rozan (0304-1111741): Counselling services
  • Taskeen (1093): Online and phone counselling
  • Pakistan Association of Mental Health
  • Karwan-e-Hayat (Karachi): Inpatient and outpatient services
  • Fountain House (Lahore): Rehabilitation support

Tips for Family and Friends

  • Do not say "just be strong" — depression is not a willpower issue
  • Listen without judgement
  • Encourage and accompany them to see a doctor or psychologist
  • Check in daily — even a brief message helps
  • Gently invite them to social activities
  • If they mention suicidal thoughts, take it seriously and do not leave them alone

Suicidal Thoughts — Act Immediately

If you or someone you care about is having suicidal thoughts:

  1. Call 1166 (Umang) or 1093 (Taskeen)
  2. Do not leave the person alone
  3. Secure medications and sharp objects in the home
  4. Go to the nearest emergency room or see a psychiatrist immediately
  5. Inform a trusted family member or friend

Common Myths in Pakistan

  • ❌ "Depression only affects wealthy people" — Completely false. It affects every social class.
  • ❌ "It means your faith is weak" — The Prophet (PBUH) and companions also experienced grief. Mental health is a separate matter.
  • ❌ "You will become addicted to the medicine" — Modern antidepressants are not addictive.
  • ❌ "Therapy is a waste of money" — It is an evidence-based, life-changing treatment.
  • ❌ "Children don't get depression" — They do, especially during teenage years.

Conclusion

Depression is treatable — but the first step is asking for help. A combination of therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes brings significant relief to 70–80% of patients. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best treatment for depression?

The most effective approach is a combination of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and medication (SSRIs such as Prozac, Sertraline, or Cipralex). Mild cases may respond well to lifestyle changes alone — exercise, sunlight, and social connection. Severe cases require psychiatrist monitoring. Results typically take 4–6 weeks. What is the difference between sadness and depression?

Sadness is a normal emotion triggered by a specific event and usually passes within a few days. Depression lasts 2+ weeks, involves a loss of interest in everything, significant changes in sleep and appetite, overwhelming hopelessness, and sometimes suicidal thoughts. Depression is a real medical illness caused by a brain chemistry imbalance — not a sign of weakness.

Are antidepressants addictive?

Modern antidepressants (SSRIs such as Prozac, Sertraline, Cipralex) are not addictive — this is a common myth. However, stopping them abruptly can cause withdrawal symptoms, so doses should be tapered gradually under medical guidance. Sleeping pills and benzodiazepines such as Lexotanil (bromazepam) can be habit-forming — use short-term only.

What are natural remedies for anxiety?

30 minutes of daily exercise (proven for anxiety), 7–8 hours of sleep, deep breathing exercises (4-7-8 technique), prayer and meditation, reducing caffeine, and avoiding social isolation all help. Magnesium and omega-3 may also be beneficial. Severe anxiety requires CBT therapy and medication. Lavender or chamomile tea can have a mild calming effect.

What should I do if I have suicidal thoughts?

Seek professional help immediately. In Pakistan, Umang (1166) and Taskeen (1093) are 24/7 helplines. Do not be alone — tell a family member or trusted friend. Secure medications and sharp objects in the home. Go to the nearest ER or see a psychiatrist urgently. These thoughts improve with treatment — you are not alone.
