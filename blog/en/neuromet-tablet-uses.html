Neuromet Tablet Uses, Dosage and Side Effects in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Neuromet = B1 (thiamine 100mg) + B6 (pyridoxine 100mg) + B12 (cyanocobalamin 200mcg) combination
  • Used for peripheral neuropathy, diabetic nerve damage, carpal tunnel syndrome and B-vitamin deficiency
  • Takes 4–8 weeks for noticeable improvement in nerve pain; full benefit may take 3–6 months
  • High-dose B6 (>200mg/day) paradoxically causes neuropathy — stay within recommended dose
  • Diabetics on metformin should routinely supplement B12 — Neuromet covers this and more

What Is Neuromet?

Neuromet is a B-vitamin complex combination tablet containing three neurotrophic vitamins: thiamine (B1) 100mg + pyridoxine (B6) 100mg + cyanocobalamin (B12) 200mcg. These three vitamins work together to support peripheral nerve function, myelin sheath integrity and nerve conduction — making Neuromet one of Pakistan's most prescribed supplements for neuropathic conditions.

Multiple brands offer the same B1+B6+B12 formula in Pakistan — including Neurobion (Merck), Tonoflex-P (AGP), and various generics. The combination is often preferred over individual B-vitamin supplements because peripheral neuropathy commonly involves deficiency or metabolic dysfunction of multiple B vitamins simultaneously.

Role of Each B Vitamin in Nerve Health

Thiamine (Vitamin B1):

Essential for glucose metabolism in neurons. B1 deficiency causes Wernicke's encephalopathy (acute) and peripheral neuropathy (chronic). In Pakistan, B1 deficiency occurs in chronic alcohol users, patients on long-term dialysis and those on a polished-rice-heavy diet. High-dose thiamine in Neuromet supports energy production in nerve cells and reduces lactic acid accumulation.

Pyridoxine (Vitamin B6):

Critical for neurotransmitter synthesis (serotonin, GABA, dopamine) and sphingolipid synthesis in the myelin sheath. B6 deficiency causes sensory neuropathy. B6 is also the key cofactor for homocysteine metabolism alongside folate and B12. Common causes of deficiency: INH (tuberculosis medication), oral contraceptives, chronic kidney disease.

Cyanocobalamin (Vitamin B12):

Essential for myelin synthesis and axonal maintenance. B12 deficiency causes subacute combined degeneration (sensory and motor neuropathy with posterior column involvement). Common causes in Pakistan: vegetarian diet, metformin use, H. pylori infection, elderly patients with reduced intrinsic factor. Note: cyanocobalamin (in Neuromet) is the standard stable form; methylcobalamin (in Methycobal) is the active neurological form and may be preferred when pure B12 deficiency is confirmed.

Neuromet Uses

1. Diabetic Peripheral Neuropathy

The most common indication in Pakistan. Diabetic neuropathy — numbness, tingling, burning pain and weakness in feet and hands — affects 50% of people with diabetes after 10+ years. All three B vitamins are frequently deficient in diabetics (especially those on metformin which depletes B12). Neuromet is given as adjunct therapy alongside blood sugar control and pain management.

2. Peripheral Neuropathy from Other Causes

B-vitamin complex is useful for neuropathy from: alcohol use disorder (thiamine deficiency is primary), chronic kidney disease, hypothyroidism, nutritional deficiency, and as adjunct therapy for any peripheral neuropathy regardless of cause.

3. Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

High-dose pyridoxine (B6) has evidence for reducing symptoms of carpal tunnel syndrome (CTS) — pain, numbness and tingling in the hand from median nerve compression at the wrist. The mechanism is thought to involve improved nerve conduction and reduced local inflammation. Neuromet's 100mg B6 per tablet provides therapeutic doses for CTS.

4. General B-Vitamin Deficiency

Fatigue, poor concentration, mouth ulcers, glossitis, and general weakness from B-vitamin deficiency respond well to Neuromet. Common in Pakistan due to inadequate dietary intake, frequent pregnancies, vegetarian diets and high tea consumption (which impairs B-vitamin absorption).

Conclusion

Neuromet (B1+B6+B12) is Pakistan's go-to nerve vitamin complex for peripheral neuropathy — especially diabetic neuropathy and B-vitamin deficiency states. It requires consistent daily use for 3–6 months to show full benefit. The combination format is practical in Pakistan where multiple B-vitamin deficiencies commonly coexist. Diabetics on metformin should routinely supplement with B12-containing products like Neuromet. Stay within recommended doses to avoid B6-induced neuropathy paradox.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Neuromet take to work?

Initial improvement in nerve pain: 4–6 weeks. Full benefit for diabetic neuropathy: 3–6 months. These vitamins repair myelin slowly — consistency is essential. Do not stop early because results are gradual.

What is the difference between Neuromet and Methycobal?

Methycobal = only methylcobalamin (active B12). Neuromet = B1 + B6 + B12 (all three neurotrophic vitamins). Neuromet is preferred when multiple deficiencies coexist (diabetes, malnutrition). Methycobal is preferred when pure B12 deficiency is confirmed and the active neurological form is needed.

Can high B6 in Neuromet cause nerve damage?

Yes — above 200mg/day long-term. Neuromet has 100mg B6 per tablet. Stay within 1–2 tablets per day and do not combine with other B6-containing supplements. Report new burning/sensory symptoms to your doctor.

Is Neuromet better taken with food?

Yes — food improves tolerability (reduces nausea from high-dose B vitamins) and slightly improves B12 absorption. Take with breakfast or any main meal for best compliance.

Can I take Neuromet with metformin?

Yes — it is strongly recommended. Metformin depletes B12 by up to 30% long-term, worsening diabetic neuropathy. Neuromet's B12 content directly counters this. All diabetics on metformin should supplement B12 routinely.
