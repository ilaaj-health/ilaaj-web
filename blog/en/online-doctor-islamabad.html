Islamabad's Healthcare Access Problem

Islamabad is Pakistan's capital, but healthcare access here isn't always easy either. Government hospitals like PIMS and Poly Clinic have long queues, private clinics are expensive, and traffic between the F-sectors and G-13 or Bahria Town can take hours. Online doctor consultation solves this problem.

When to Go to the Hospital in an Emergency

For serious emergencies in Islamabad, go immediately to PIMS Emergency, Shifa International, or Kulsum International — chest pain, severe bleeding, breathing difficulty, or stroke symptoms are not appropriate for online consultation. Call 1122.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is an online doctor's prescription legally valid in Islamabad?

Yes, on Ilaaj AI every prescription is reviewed and approved by a licensed PMDC doctor — making it legally valid and fillable at any pharmacy in Islamabad.

How quickly can I get an online consultation in Islamabad?

The entire process — describing symptoms, AI assessment, and doctor approval — takes about 15-20 minutes on average. It is not a substitute for emergency care.

Is online consultation available for children in Islamabad?

Yes, through family profiles you can also get consultations for children — ideal for common paediatric issues like fever, cough, and allergies.

Which pharmacies in Islamabad accept these prescriptions?

Any licensed pharmacy — Chase Pharmacy, D-Watson, Servaid, or your local medical store — since the prescription is PMDC-verified.

Should I use an online doctor during an emergency in Islamabad?

Absolutely not — for chest pain, severe bleeding, breathing difficulty, or stroke symptoms, call 1122 immediately or go to the nearest hospital (PIMS, Shifa International). Online consultation is only for non-emergency conditions.
