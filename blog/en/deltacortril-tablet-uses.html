Deltacortril Uses — Complete Prednisolone Treatment Guide

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Deltacortril contains prednisolone — a powerful corticosteroid
  • Used for allergies, asthma, arthritis, and autoimmune conditions
  • Stopping suddenly is dangerous — the dose must be tapered gradually
  • Long-term use risks weight gain, bone thinning, and raised blood sugar
  • Never self-medicate — doctor supervision is essential

What Is Deltacortril?

Deltacortril contains the active ingredient prednisolone — a powerful corticosteroid that mimics the body's natural cortisol hormone. It reduces inflammation and calms an overactive immune system.

What Is Deltacortril Used For?

  • Severe allergic reactions — after anaphylaxis, severe skin allergies
  • Asthma flare-ups — when an inhaler alone isn't enough
  • Rheumatoid arthritis and other autoimmune joint diseases
  • Inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's, Ulcerative colitis)
  • Lupus and other autoimmune conditions
  • Severe skin conditions — pemphigus, severe eczema
  • Nephrotic syndrome (kidney disease)
  • Part of treatment for certain cancers

Dosage

Dosing depends on the condition's severity and type — always follow your doctor's prescribed dose. Common range is 5-60mg per day: higher for short-term flare-ups, lower for long-term maintenance.

CRITICAL: Tapering Warning

If Deltacortril has been taken for more than 5 days, stopping it suddenly is dangerous. Steroid use suppresses the body's own cortisol production — stopping abruptly can trigger an adrenal crisis:

  • Severe fatigue
  • Blood pressure crash
  • Nausea, vomiting
  • Confusion or loss of consciousness

The dose must always be tapered gradually following your doctor's schedule — never decide to stop on your own.

Side Effects

Short-term:

  • Increased appetite, weight gain
  • Mood changes, insomnia
  • Raised blood sugar
  • Stomach irritation

Long-term use risks:

  • Osteoporosis (weakened bones)
  • New-onset or worsened diabetes
  • Weight gain, "moon face"
  • Weakened immune system — increased infection risk
  • High blood pressure
  • Cataracts
  • Thin skin, easy bruising

Who Should Be Cautious

  • Patients with diabetes — monitor blood sugar
  • Those at risk of osteoporosis
  • Patients with an active infection
  • History of peptic ulcer
  • Glaucoma or cataract patients

Pregnancy and Breastfeeding

Deltacortril can be used in pregnancy in some cases when clearly necessary, under close doctor supervision. Do not take it on your own without medical guidance.

Conclusion

Deltacortril (prednisolone) is highly effective for severe conditions, but requires strict doctor supervision and proper tapering. Stopping suddenly or changing the dose yourself can be dangerous. Discuss your exact dose and tapering schedule with a doctor on Ilaaj AI.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I stop Deltacortril suddenly?

Not after using it for more than 5 days — stopping suddenly can trigger an adrenal crisis (blood pressure crash, severe fatigue, loss of consciousness). The dose must always be tapered gradually per your doctor's schedule.

How long should Deltacortril be used for?

This depends on the condition — some allergic reactions need only 5-7 days, while autoimmune conditions may need weeks or months. Always follow the doctor's prescribed course and don't extend the duration yourself.

Why does Deltacortril cause weight gain?

Prednisolone increases appetite and causes fluid retention, leading to weight gain — especially in the face and abdomen ('moon face'). This is reversible once the dose is reduced, and can be managed with diet control.

Can I exercise while taking Deltacortril?

Yes, light to moderate exercise is fine, but be cautious with high-impact activities due to reduced bone density risk. Discuss guidance specific to your condition with your doctor.

Why does Deltacortril increase infection risk?

Prednisolone suppresses the immune system to reduce inflammation — which also makes the body less able to fight off infections. Report any fever, sore throat, or unusual symptom to your doctor immediately.
