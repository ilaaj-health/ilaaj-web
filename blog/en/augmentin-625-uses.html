Augmentin 625mg — Uses, Dosage and Side Effects Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • Augmentin = Amoxicillin 500 mg + Clavulanic Acid 125 mg
  • Effective only against bacterial infections — useless for viral illnesses
  • Always complete the full course — stopping early causes resistance
  • Avoid if you have a penicillin allergy
  • Take with food — reduces stomach upset and improves absorption

What Is Augmentin 625?

Augmentin is GlaxoSmithKline's brand name for the amoxicillin + clavulanic acid combination. It is a broad-spectrum antibiotic effective against a wide range of bacterial infections, and is one of the most commonly prescribed antibiotics in Pakistan.

Augmentin 625 — Quick Facts
CompositionAmoxicillin 500 mg + Clavulanic Acid 125 mg
ClassBeta-lactam antibiotic (penicillin family)
ManufacturerGlaxoSmithKline
FormTablets, syrup (children), IV injection
Prescription onlyYes — it is an antibiotic
Price in Pakistan~700–1000 PKR for 10 tablets

Why Is Clavulanic Acid Added?

Many bacteria have developed a beta-lactamase enzyme that destroys amoxicillin. Clavulanic acid blocks this enzyme, allowing Augmentin to work against bacteria that would otherwise be resistant to plain amoxicillin. This is what gives it an "extended spectrum".

What Infections Does Augmentin 625 Treat?

1. Respiratory Infections

  • Sinusitis (sinus infection)
  • Bacterial bronchitis
  • Community-acquired pneumonia
  • Strep throat
  • Bacterial tonsillitis
  • Otitis media (middle ear infection)

2. Urinary Tract Infection (UTI)

  • Cystitis (bladder infection)
  • Pyelonephritis (kidney infection) — IV form for severe cases

3. Skin and Soft Tissue Infections

  • Cellulitis
  • Wound infections
  • Animal and human bites
  • Diabetic foot ulcers
  • Boils and abscesses

4. Dental Infections

  • Tooth abscess
  • Gum infection (periodontitis)
  • Post-extraction infections

5. Other Uses

  • Severe bacterial gastroenteritis
  • H. pylori eradication (as part of combination therapy)
  • Surgical prophylaxis

Dosage

For Adults and Children 12 and Over:

  • Mild-moderate infections: 1 tablet (625 mg) twice daily
  • Severe infections: 1 tablet three times daily, or 1000 mg twice daily
  • Course duration: 5–14 days depending on the infection
  • Take with food — better absorption and less stomach upset

For Children (Augmentin Syrup):

Dose depends on weight (25–45 mg/kg/day):

  • 156 mg/5 ml strength: 5–15 ml twice daily
  • 228 mg/5 ml strength: 5–10 ml twice daily
  • 457 mg/5 ml strength: 2.5–10 ml twice daily

Duration by Condition:

  • Strep throat: 10 days
  • UTI: 5–7 days
  • Sinusitis: 7–10 days
  • Pneumonia: 7–14 days
  • Dental abscess: 5–7 days

Critical: Always Complete the Full Course

Antibiotic resistance is a major public health crisis in Pakistan. Stopping a course early because you feel better leads to:

  • Bacteria developing resistance to the antibiotic
  • Antibiotics failing to work in future infections
  • The infection returning in a stronger form
  • Pakistan already faces XDR typhoid — a direct result of this problem

Always complete the full course — even if you feel completely well after 3 days.

Side Effects

Common (10–30% of users):

  • Loose stools or diarrhoea (most common)
  • Nausea, vomiting
  • Stomach pain
  • Mild skin rash
  • Vaginal yeast infection (in women)

Serious:

  • Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis): Difficulty breathing, swelling — emergency
  • C. difficile colitis: Severe diarrhoea after antibiotic use
  • Stevens-Johnson syndrome: Severe skin reaction
  • Liver damage: Jaundice, dark urine
  • Blood disorders: Reduced platelets or white blood cells

When to Avoid Augmentin

  • Penicillin allergy — extremely dangerous
  • History of liver problems caused by Augmentin
  • Infectious mononucleosis (glandular fever) — causes a widespread rash
  • Severe kidney impairment — dose adjustment required

Drug Interactions

  • Oral contraceptive pills: May reduce effectiveness — use backup contraception
  • Warfarin: Increased bleeding risk
  • Methotrexate: Toxicity
  • Allopurinol: Increased risk of rash
  • Probenecid: Raises Augmentin blood levels

When Is Augmentin Useless?

Augmentin has absolutely no effect on viral infections:

  • Common cold
  • Most cases of flu
  • Most sore throats (90% are viral)
  • Most diarrhoea (mostly viral)
  • COVID-19
  • Bronchitis (mostly viral in adults)

Taking an antibiotic for these conditions is harmful — it achieves nothing while causing side effects and contributing to antibiotic resistance.

Take Probiotics Alongside

Augmentin kills beneficial gut bacteria as well as harmful ones. Take yoghurt or a probiotic supplement (Enterogermina, Bifilac) alongside your course, with at least a 2-hour gap from the antibiotic dose.

Pregnancy and Breastfeeding

  • Pregnancy: Generally safe (FDA Category B). Use with doctor's prescription.
  • Breastfeeding: Safe — a small amount passes to the baby
  • The baby may develop mild diarrhoea, which is not harmful

Brands and Prices in Pakistan

  • Augmentin (GSK) — premium brand
  • Calamox (Highnoon)
  • Camox (Sami)
  • Klaract (CCL)
  • Generic amoxicillin-clavulanate options

All contain the same drug. Generic versions are often half the price with similar quality.

Common Misuse in Pakistan

  • Buying from pharmacies without a prescription
  • Taking it for colds and flu (viral)
  • Stopping the course after 3 days when feeling better
  • Giving leftover antibiotics to another person
  • Changing the dose or duration from what the doctor prescribed

Antibiotic Resistance — Pakistan's Crisis

Antibiotic overuse in Pakistan is so widespread that XDR typhoid has emerged — a strain that does not respond to standard antibiotics. Augmentin is also gradually becoming less effective. Responsible use is a responsibility we all share.

Conclusion

Augmentin 625 is a broad-spectrum antibiotic effective for respiratory, UTI, skin, and dental bacterial infections in Pakistan. Always obtain a prescription, complete the full course, and never use it for viral illnesses. Avoid it completely with a penicillin allergy. Frequently Asked Questions

What is Augmentin 625 used for?

Augmentin 625 (amoxicillin + clavulanic acid) treats bacterial infections: sinusitis, bronchitis, pneumonia, strep throat, tonsillitis, ear infections, UTIs, skin infections, dental abscesses, and bites. Effective only against bacterial infections — useless against viral illnesses such as colds, flu, and COVID-19. Requires a prescription; complete the full course.

What is the correct dosage of Augmentin 625?

For adults with mild-to-moderate infections: 1 tablet (625 mg) twice daily. For severe infections: three times daily or 1000 mg twice daily. Take with food — better absorption and less stomach upset. Course duration depends on the infection (UTI 5–7 days, strep 10 days, pneumonia 7–14 days). Children use Augmentin syrup dosed by weight.

What are Augmentin's side effects?

Common (10–30%): diarrhoea or loose stools (most common), nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, mild rash, vaginal yeast infection in women. Serious: severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis — emergency), C. difficile colitis (severe post-antibiotic diarrhoea), Stevens-Johnson syndrome (skin reaction), liver damage. Take probiotics (yoghurt or Enterogermina) to protect gut bacteria.

Is Augmentin safe during pregnancy?

Yes, Augmentin is generally safe in pregnancy (FDA Category B) — but only with a doctor's prescription. Commonly used for UTIs, respiratory infections, and dental infections in pregnant women. Also safe while breastfeeding — a small amount passes to the baby. The baby may develop mild diarrhoea, which is harmless. Avoid completely if you have a penicillin allergy.

Can I stop Augmentin early if I feel better?

Absolutely not. Complete the full course even when symptoms resolve. Stopping early allows bacteria to develop resistance, making antibiotics ineffective in future illnesses. Pakistan's XDR typhoid crisis developed partly for this reason. Always follow the duration prescribed by your doctor — even if you feel well after 3 days, complete the 7–10 day course.
