Stomach Pain Treatment — Causes, Home Remedies and Best Medicine in Pakistan

TL;DR — Key Takeaways
  • The most common causes of stomach pain are gas, acidity, and food poisoning
  • For mild stomach pain, try warm water, ginger, and buttermilk first
  • Risek 20 or an antacid is effective for acidity
  • It is essential to give ORS to children with loose motions
  • Severe pain, vomiting blood, or pain lasting more than 24 hours is an emergency

Common Causes of Stomach Pain

Stomach pain (abdominal pain) is one of the most common reasons people visit a doctor in Pakistan. Heat, spoiled food, contaminated water, and stress all affect the digestive system. But not all stomach pain is the same: some resolve in 30 minutes, while others require hospital treatment.

Stomach Pain — Common Causes
Gas / BloatingStomach swelling after eating, passing wind
Acidity / GERDBurning after eating, sour burping
Food PoisoningContaminated street food, with vomiting and diarrhea
UlcerPain on empty stomach, worsened by spicy food
Period CrampsLower abdominal pain in women during menstruation
AppendicitisSevere pain in lower right abdomen — emergency

Immediate Home Remedies for Stomach Pain

1. Drink Warm Water

A glass of warm water relaxes gas and cramps. Making a habit of drinking warm water on an empty stomach in the morning also reduces acidity.

2. Ginger

Chewing a small piece of ginger makes a difference for nausea and stomach upset. You can also try ginger tea.

3. Mint Tea

Mint (pudina) is a time-tested remedy for gas and bloating. Boil 5–6 leaves in hot water for 5 minutes.

4. Asafoetida (Hing) and Warm Water

Dissolving a pinch of asafoetida in warm water and drinking it provides quick relief from gas — a traditional Pakistani home remedy passed down through generations.

5. Heating Pad or Warm Towel

Apply a warm pad or hot water bottle to your stomach for 15–20 minutes — very effective for period cramps and muscle pain.

6. Yogurt (Dahi)

Probiotic yogurt is helpful for both acidity and loose motions. Take 1 cup of yogurt on an empty stomach.

Common Stomach Pain Medicines in Pakistan

These medicines are available over the counter but should not be used for more than 2 days without a doctor's advice:

  • Acidity / GERD: Risek 20 mg (omeprazole), Pantop 40 mg, or a simple antacid (Gaviscon, Mylanta)
  • Gas / Bloating: Disflatyl, Buscopan
  • Loose Motions: ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts) — most essential. Imodium for adults only — do not give to children
  • Period Cramps: Ponstan 250 mg or Brufen 400 mg
  • Vomiting: Motilium 10 mg or Buscopan
  • Spasms / Cramps: Buscopan tablet

Stomach Pain in Children

For stomach pain in children, especially check the following:

  • With loose motions, give ORS — 1 cup after every bowel movement
  • For breastfed babies with gas, give a gentle stomach massage
  • Do not give Imodium or Brufen to children under 5 on your own
  • If stomach pain is accompanied by fever, always see a doctor
  • If the child stops feeding, becomes lethargic, or is urinating less — take them to hospital

Acidity (GERD) Treatment

In Pakistan, tea, spicy food, and late-night dinners are the biggest causes of acidity. For long-term treatment:

  • Finish eating at least 3 hours before bedtime
  • Slightly elevate your head when sleeping (extra pillow)
  • Completely stop smoking and chewing paan/gutka
  • Eat small meals 4–5 times a day instead of 2 large ones
  • Avoid sour, spicy, and fried foods
  • Reduce tea, coffee, and soft drinks

When to See a Doctor Immediately

  • Severe pain in the lower right abdomen (suspected appendicitis)
  • Vomiting blood or black stool
  • Pain so intense you cannot walk
  • Stomach pain with fever lasting more than 24 hours
  • Stomach pain in pregnant women
  • Loose motions for more than 3 days (risk of dehydration)
  • Urination stops or eyes turn yellow (jaundice)

Prevention — Daily Habits for Pakistan

  • Drink 8 glasses of water daily
  • Avoid uncovered street food, especially in summer
  • Get fibre from fruits and vegetables — helps with both constipation and acidity
  • Chew your food properly (at least 20 chews per bite)
  • Do not lie down immediately after eating
  • Include yogurt or buttermilk in your daily diet

Conclusion

Treating stomach pain starts with understanding its cause. Mild pain often gets better with home remedies, but do not ignore severe pain or pain lasting more than 24 hours. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best medicine for stomach pain in Pakistan?

It depends on the cause. For acidity, Risek 20 mg or an antacid (Gaviscon) works well. For gas, Disflatyl is effective. For period cramps, Ponstan 250 mg or Brufen 400 mg. For loose motions, ORS (Oral Rehydration Salts) should be given first. Do not use painkillers for more than 2 days without a doctor's advice.

What is a quick home remedy for stomach pain?

Drink warm water, try ginger or mint tea, dissolve a pinch of asafoetida (hing) in warm water, and place a warm compress on your stomach. These methods can make a difference within 30 minutes for gas, bloating, and mild cramps. If there is no improvement after 1 hour, take medicine.

What does pain on the right side of the stomach indicate?

Severe pain in the lower right abdomen can be a sign of appendicitis — this is a medical emergency and you must go to the hospital immediately. Pain in the upper right side may be related to the liver or gallbladder. Never ignore pain that persists on one side for a long time.

How to treat stomach pain in children?

For children, first try a gentle stomach massage and a warm compress. If there are loose motions, give ORS after every bowel movement. Do not give Imodium, Brufen, or antibiotics to children under 5 on your own. Stomach pain with fever, lethargy, or stopping feeding are emergency signs — see a doctor immediately.

What is the immediate remedy for gas in the stomach?

Mix a pinch of asafoetida (hing) or lemon juice in warm water and drink it. Mint tea also reduces gas within 10–15 minutes. Instead of sitting for a long time, take a 5–10 minute light walk. For the long term, reduce spicy, fried, and carbonated drinks. Eat yogurt daily.
